## What this is about

**The system, in one paragraph (for a first read).** This is the R=CΨ² project's open spin chain: N spins with exchange coupling J, under local Z-dephasing at rate γ (the "observation"). Its evolution is generated by a *Liouvillian* (the dissipative analogue of a Hamiltonian), whose eigenvalues λ are complex: Re λ is a relaxation rate (how fast a mode fades), Im λ an oscillation frequency. One dimensionless knob, **q = J/γ** (coupling vs observation), tunes everything; promoted to a complex variable it exposes the analytic structure. We zoom into one invariant piece of that spectrum, the **(SE,DE) coherence block**: the density-matrix coherences linking the single-excitation sector (SE, one spin flipped) to the double-excitation sector (DE, two flipped). As q is tuned, two eigenvalues of this block can collide, and the *kind* of collision is the whole story.

**Two kinds of collision.** A **diabolic** point is a genuine crossing: the two eigenvalues coincide but their eigenvectors stay independent (*semisimple*), and it is *silent* (no square-root singularity). A **defective** collision, an *exceptional point* (EP), is the opposite: the eigenvectors coalesce into a Jordan block (a √-branch), and it is *loud* (amplified). Telling these apart at every collision is what the instrument below does.

**Terms used here** (project labels, one line each): *path-k* is the N=(k+1)-site chain (path-3 = N=4, path-4 = N=5); *the AT* is the [Absorption Theorem](../docs/proofs/PROOF_ABSORPTION_THEOREM.md) Re λ = −2γ⟨n_XY⟩, which fixes every relaxation rate from a mode's "light content" ⟨n_XY⟩ (how many of its sites carry an X or Y rather than an I or Z; the X/Y letters are the ones Z-dephasing actually damps, so more of them means faster fade); *the residual* is the part of the block left after removing the AT-locked closed-form strands (the part carrying the interesting structure), with *F_d* its polynomial factor of degree d and a *strand* one eigenvalue branch of the block as q is tuned; *the self-fold* is an antiunitary mirror symmetry the block has only at N=4 (the cause of the N=4 diabolic's specialness, below); *Tier 1/2* are the project's confidence labels (1 = proven or machine-verified, 2 = computed or plausible); *an arc* is an open research thread in the ledger; *R-1/R-2* are the two risk arguments from the reviewed investigation plan, summarized inline where used.

At N=4 (the path-3 chain) the (SE,DE) block has a single silent **diabolic** at a physical coupling q = q_EP ≈ 0.659, at λ_EP = −4 + 2iJ (rate −4 in units of γ, frequency 2J). The [zeros_connecting_structure](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Core/OpenArcs/OpenArcsRegistry.cs) arc traced it from below ([`gmscan --trace`](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Diagnostics/Foundation/GaloisMonodromyWitness.cs)): a mirror pair of modes slides together and merges on the symmetry line (the *fold*). That picture is N=4-only, because the within-block self-fold exists only at N=4 (the half-filling coincidence where the DE two-excitation sector is its own bit-flip complement, which happens only when the 2 excitations equal the N−2 empties, i.e. N=4). The forward question: **does an analogue survive to N=5 (path-4), or does removing the self-fold kill the diabolic?**

The honest prior (the reviewed investigation plan's R-1/R-2): a diabolic is a *node* of the spectral curve, codim-3-complex = 6 real conditions, overdetermined by 4 in a 2-real-DOF complex-q scan, so **generically absent unless a symmetry auto-satisfies it**. At N=4 the self-fold (plus the reality of q_EP) did exactly that. Remove it at N≥5 and the generic expectation is: no diabolic. The answer turned out richer than either "yes" or "no", and it reconciles the codimension argument with integrability: the diabolics **survive at every N** (free-fermion integrability keeps the crossings), but **where they sit in q moves**: all at complex q for N=5 and N=6, then several **returning to the physical real axis by N=7** (see the path-5 and path-6 sections).

## The instrument, validated at path-3

[`pkmono --diabolic`](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Cli/Commands/PathKMonodromyScanCommand.cs) (engine: [`PathKMonodromyScout.FindDiabolics`](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Diagnostics/Foundation/PathKMonodromyScout.cs)) hunts the residual block's coalescences: it scans the complex-q plane for full-block min-gap minima (no per-q AT stripping, which is valid only at q=2; the residual SET is tracked from q=2 by continuity, since F_AT·F_residual are distinct factors), then classifies each candidate by three independent signals:

- the **monodromy loop** (a small q-loop: identity ⟹ no √-branch ⟹ diabolic candidate; transposition ⟹ defective EP),
- the **gap-scaling exponent** (gap ∝ |q−q\*|^p: p ≈ 1 linear ⟹ two sheets crossing ⟹ diabolic; p ≈ ½ ⟹ defective √-branch),
- the **EpCharacter** verdict (Riesz projector: geo = alg, departure ≈ 0 ⟹ semisimple/diabolic; geo < alg ⟹ defective Jordan block).

At path-3 the three agree and reproduce the **known** diabolic structure of the squared discriminant factor (3q⁴+q²−1)² exactly: the real pair q ≈ ±0.659 (λ = −4 ± 1.318i) **and** the imaginary pair q ≈ ±0.876i (λ = −2.248 / −5.752), all identity-loop, exponent 1.00; the genuine EPs are correctly transposition-loop, exponent ½. This is the [`PathKDiabolicTests`](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Diagnostics.Tests/Foundation/PathKDiabolicTests.cs) gate (the path-k path reproduces `OcticRootsAt`; the classifier re-finds q_EP as diabolic and a generic eigenvalue as not).

## The path-5 (N=6) result, and the residual-only instrument

Pushing the same hunt to path-5 (N=6) first broke the instrument, then confirmed the pattern. The naive `pkmono --diabolic --k 5` over the path-4 box floods: 528 coalescences, every one with gap exactly zero and residual=False (the scan's per-coalescence flag: the coalescing pair is AT-locked, not part of the residual). These are not diabolics but the AT-locked strands crossing each other. At N=6 the double-excitation sector carries enough repeated light-content ⟨n_XY⟩ that many AT strands share an identical relaxation rate Re λ = −2γ⟨n_XY⟩, so a pair coincides (Re and Im both equal) wherever their frequencies cross, which happens on dense curves throughout complex q. The full-block min-gap scan that was clean at N=5 (zero such crossings in the box) drowns at N=6, and the genuine residual coalescences, sitting at a small but finite gap, are never even local minima of a field that is zero around them.

The fix is to scan the residual strands only. [`ResidualRootsTracked`](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Diagnostics/Foundation/PathKMonodromyScout.cs) follows the residual SET by nearest-neighbour continuity from the base q0 = 2 (where it is identified exactly against the reconstructed AT factor); the SET is monodromy-invariant because F_AT and F_residual are distinct factors, so it survives any label permutations on the path. With the AT strands gone from the root set, the gap field, the refinement, the monodromy loop and the gap-scaling exponent all see only the H_B-mixed residual. The locator is exposed as `pkmono --diabolic --residual --k N`. Two rounds of a from-q0 re-tracking nest had to be removed for it to scale: `GapRefineResidualLocal` and `ResidualLoopIsIdentity` track locally from the candidate instead of globally per probe, dropping the [path-k diabolic test suite](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Diagnostics.Tests/Foundation/PathKDiabolicTests.cs) from 11 minutes to 15 seconds.

With the residual-only scan, the same box re[0.2,3] × im[−1.5,1.5] at cell 0.05 gives 21 clean coalescences: **16 semisimple residual diabolics and 5 defective EPs** (path-4 gave 11 + 4). Two things hold, exactly as at path-4:

- **The count grows with N**: 11 (N=5) → 16 (N=6), consistent with the integrable level-crossing density rising as the residual degree F_d climbs (18 → 32).
- **Complex-q only**: the diabolics at re(q) = 0 sit on the imaginary axis (an analytic continuation, like path-3's ±0.876i); the rest are at complex q; the ones nearest the physical axis (im(q) ≈ 0.01 to 0.05) are the ghosts of the path-4 kind (q = 0.6118 ± 0.012i there), split off the axis rather than on it. None sits at a physical real q. The self-fold that put one diabolic on the real axis was N=4-only, and nothing brings a diabolic back to the physical axis at N=6.

**The Δ-test confirms integrability-protection at N=6.** The mechanism question (does XXZ anisotropy kill the diabolics, as it does at N=4?) needed the same residual treatment: the full-block Δ-test box scan is captured by the AT crossings at N=6, so q* jumps to an exact-zero AT degeneracy and the verdict turns unreliable (it reads "integrability-protected" for the wrong reason). With a residual-aware tracker ([`ResidualRootsTrackedXxz`](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Diagnostics/Foundation/XxzCoherenceBlock.cs); the residual SET is Δ-stable because the ZZ term is Hermitian, so the AT rate Re λ = −2γ⟨n_XY⟩ is Δ-independent), each path-5 diabolic reads cleanly: diabolic (geometric multiplicity = algebraic multiplicity = 2, the two eigenvectors stay independent) at Δ = 0 with q* pinned at the seed and a finite gap, then DEFECTIVE (geometric drops to 1 < algebraic 2, a Jordan block) the instant Δ ≠ 0, the departure-from-normality growing with Δ. The rung-near diabolic at q = 0.709 − 0.219i (which the full-block test captured entirely, reading false LIFTs at a wandering q*) now flips clean: departure 0.028 → 0.073 → 0.160 across Δ = 0.02, 0.05, 0.10, q* never leaving the seed. The free-fermion integrability that creates the diabolics protects them at N=6 exactly as at N=4, via `pkmono --delta-flip --residual`.

Honest limits. The 16 is the EpCharacter count (the load-bearing R-3 discriminant) in this box at this resolution; completeness is not claimed (obstacle 1, as at path-4). A few coalescences read diabolic by EpCharacter and the identity loop but show a gap-scaling exponent near ½ rather than 1 (for example q = 0.6508 + 0.052i, exponent 0.49); these need the per-point three-signal reconciliation the path-4 near-axis pairs needed, and are not yet individually settled. Pushing to path-6 (N=7) then broke the residual-only tracker itself, which is the next section.

## The path-6 (N=7) result: the exact-residual instrument, and real-q diabolics appear

Pushing the hunt to path-6 (N=7) first exposed that the residual-only **tracker** does not survive the strand density of N=7's residual factor (F_53: 53 residual strands, up from 18 and 32 at N=5 and N=6), then, once that was fixed, turned up something the lower N did not have.

**Why the tracked instrument broke (not "just slow").** The residual-only path of path-5 identifies the residual strands by a nearest-match removal of the reconstructed AT factor from the numerical block spectrum, then follows that SET by nearest-neighbour continuity from q0 = 2. At N=7 the **S₂-symmetric (SE,DE) block** (the symmetry-reduced block the scout works on, not the full 147-dimensional coherence block) is 75-dimensional (= 22 AT + 53 residual) and carries exact rate-degeneracies: many strands sit at exactly Re λ = −6 (the AT rate for ⟨n_XY⟩ = 3, the maximally-damped sector, where the coherence's bra and ket share no excited site), several coinciding. The 22-root AT factor cannot tell which of these degenerate strands are AT, so a chunk of genuine AT strands (and exact duplicates) leak into the "residual" SET already at the base q0 = 2. The tell-tale: three strands all at exactly −6.000 + 0i, i.e. a triply-repeated root. The true residual factor is a degree-53 polynomial whose roots are all distinct (it is *separable*, the condition that makes it a clean Galois factor), so it *cannot* have a repeated root; three coincident strands therefore prove that at least two of them are leaked-in AT strands wearing a residual label, not genuine residual roots. Their exact same-rate degeneracies make the residual min-gap zero across the whole box, so a broad scan reports hundreds of spurious "gap = 0, exponent = NaN" coalescences (293 in the re[0.2,3] × im[−1.5,1.5] box) and isolates no genuine ones. This is *not* the per-evaluation-cost / wall-clock obstacle the path-5 notes anticipated; the per-evaluation result is corrupt, and a smaller box or seed pre-filter would have produced the same artifact.

**The fix: the residual roots EXACTLY, with no partition and no tracking.** In plain words: instead of guessing strand-by-strand which are AT and chasing them across the plane, carve off the entire AT-carrying subspace once (it is the same at every q) and read the eigenvalues of what is left. The algebra makes that exact. Write the block matrix as **M(q)** (the (SE,DE) block at coupling q); it splits as M(q) = D/2 + i·q·K/2, where **D** is the q-independent dephasing rate diagonal (the relaxation rates, fixed by the observation) and **K** is the real-symmetric hopping (the Hamiltonian coupling). The AT-locked strands span an invariant subspace W_AT of M(q), and that subspace is **q-independent**: it is built from the *rate sectors* (groups of strands sharing one D-rate) and the K-invariant structure of the hopping, and K-invariance does not change when K is scaled by q. So the residual roots at any q are exactly the eigenvalues of M(q) compressed onto the **orthogonal complement** of W_AT: with an orthonormal split [U_AT | U_res] (a full QR of the AT invariant-subspace basis), the compression is block-triangular (U_resᴴ M(q) U_AT = 0 because W_AT is M(q)-invariant), so eig(U_resᴴ M(q) U_res) = the full spectrum minus the AT part = the F_d residual roots, AT-free by construction. No nearest-match, no continuity tracking, a single F_d × F_d eigendecomposition per q. The AT invariant subspace is exactly the one whose characteristic polynomial [`F89AtFactorReconstruction.ForPathK`](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Core/F89PathK/F89AtFactorReconstruction.cs) already returns as the validated AT factor (the full-D reconstruction test asserts it equals the committed oracle for k = 4,5,6), so eig(U_resᴴ M U_res) are the genuine oracle F_d roots. Exposed as [`PathKMonodromyScout.ResidualRootsExact` / `FindDiabolicsExact`](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Diagnostics/Foundation/PathKMonodromyScout.cs) and the CLI flag `pkmono --diabolic --exact`. It re-gates the established science exactly: the same boxes give **11 diabolics at N=5 and 16 at N=6** (matching the tracked path), and the residual roots are distinct (min gap ≫ 0) where the tracked path flooded with duplicates. As a bonus it is several times faster (one small EVD per cell instead of a 160-step continuity track of the full block).

**The count grows, and grows fast.** The same broad box re[0.2,3] × im[−1.5,1.5] at cell 0.05 gives **37 semisimple residual diabolics** at N=7, all with finite refined gaps (~10⁻⁸ to 10⁻¹⁰), gap-scaling exponent ≈ 1, identity loop, and varied λ, mostly in q-conjugate pairs:

| N | residual degree F_d | in-region diabolics (cell 0.05) |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | 18 | 11 |
| 6 | 32 | 16 |
| 7 | 53 | 37 |

(In-region EpCharacter counts at this resolution; completeness is not claimed, as at every N. A finer real-axis strip at cell 0.01 finds more, the count being resolution-dependent.)

**The new thing: diabolics appear at PHYSICAL REAL q at N=7.** At N=4 a diabolic sat at real q (the self-fold's doing); at N=5 and N=6 none did (only defective EPs live on the real axis there). At N=7 the exact scan finds **semisimple diabolics at real q and real λ**: q = 1.1264, 1.3038, 2.6280 in the cell-0.05 broad box (λ = −4.942, −5.171, −4.343), plus a fourth, q = 0.6788 (λ = −4.557), in a finer cell-0.01 real-axis strip. These are verified genuine: a tight zoom (cell 0.002) on two of them (q = 1.1264 and q = 2.6280) keeps each a single on-axis point with refined gap ~10⁻⁹ that does **not** split into a conjugate pair (unlike the path-4 ghost at 0.6118 ± 0.012i, which the same fine scan resolves as off-axis); the other two read as single on-axis points at the cell-0.01 strip but were not separately tight-zoomed. And it is a real N=7 onset, not a tracked-method miss: the exact instrument run on the N=5 and N=6 real-axis strips finds **no** real-q diabolic there either (N=5 keeps only defective EPs on the real axis, q ≈ 0.6209 and q ≈ 1.0776 in the octic story), reproducing the established lower-N result. So the simple reading "the self-fold is N=4-only ⟹ complex-q only for all N ≥ 5" is too strong: a different mechanism returns diabolics to the physical axis by N=7. Whether that return is a threshold, an even/odd parity effect, or a one-off is now **settled by the N=8 / N=9 discriminator** (next section): it is **parity-gated, odd N ≥ 7** (N=8 even has none, N=9 odd has them again). The *cause* of that parity is grounded from below (the dimension-mismatch / sector-swap, next section). The integrability character of these real-q diabolics, by contrast, is now settled: the Δ-test (does an XXZ anisotropy flip them defective, as it does the complex-q ones?) is run by porting the exact-residual treatment to the [`XxzCoherenceBlock`](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Diagnostics/Foundation/XxzCoherenceBlock.cs), whose tracked residual path breaks at k = 6 for the same reason (the F_53 AT-degeneracy flood). The answer is clean and it is reported in the Δ-test section below: all four real-q diabolics flip defective or lift the instant Δ ≠ 0, the same integrability-protected death as the complex-q ones. The real-q diabolics are a **characterized species**: integrable level-crossings, placed on the physical axis by the N=7 mechanism but protected by the same free-fermion structure as every other diabolic in this story.

## The real-q onset is an odd-N effect: the N=8 / N=9 discriminator

The N=7 onset left one sharp question: is the return of diabolics to the physical axis a **threshold** (every N ≥ 7 has them), an **even/odd** parity effect, or a **one-off** quirk of N=7? Two more N settle it, and the answer is parity.

**No rebuild was needed.** The exact-residual instrument is already N-general: the AT invariant subspace is reconstructed from the rate sectors at any block size ([`AtInvariantSubspaceBasis`](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Core/F89PathK/F89AtFactorReconstruction.cs)), and `pkmono --diabolic --exact --k N` builds the (SE,DE) block at nBlock = k+1 for any k. (Only the *test* oracle [`F89PathKFdOracle`](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Core/F89PathK/F89PathKFdOracle.cs), the committed F_d literals used to cross-check the residual count, stops at k = 6; the scan itself does not read it.) So N=8 (k=7) and N=9 (k=8) are a **run**, not a build: the same matched real-axis strip (re[0.2,3], a thin im band, cell 0.05 then cell 0.01) and the same tight-zoom split-test that confirmed the N=7 real-q diabolics.

**N=8 (even): none.** The strip's near-axis coalescences all sit *off* the real axis. The closest real-λ candidate, q = 0.6904, refines to im(q) = −0.004 (not 0) inside a dense cluster of partial strand-crossings (gap-scaling exponents scattered 0.1–0.7, none the clean linear ≈ 1 of a true crossing; best gap stuck at ~10⁻⁸). A tight zoom (cell 0.002) over the whole candidate band re[0.58, 0.73] finds **no** isolated on-axis point with real λ: the only exact-im(q)=0 point there carries a complex λ (q = 0.6165, λ = −4.617 + 1.497i), and the real-λ near-candidates never reach the axis. The genuine N=7-type diabolic (real q **and** real λ, isolated, gap → 10⁻⁹) is absent.

### The mechanism, grounded from below: the dimension-mismatch / sector-swap

The reflection R: i ↦ N−1−i commutes with the block (it is the S₂ the block is built on, in [`F89PathKSeDeBlock`](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Core/F89PathK/F89PathKSeDeBlock.cs)) and splits the full (SE,DE) coherence block into an R-even and an R-odd sector. The realness symmetry at real q (the chiral antiunitarity Σ L Σ = L†, which forces the spectrum real-or-conjugate-paired) has a fixed relation to R, and that relation flips with the parity of N. This is computed from below by building the full (SE,DE) block, splitting it by R, and measuring whether the antiunitarity acts within each sector (self-conjugate) or across them:

- **At even N** the two sectors have equal dimension and the antiunitarity maps one onto the other: σ(R-even) = conj(σ(R-odd)) **exactly** (the cross-sector conjugacy defect is ~10⁻¹³, while each sector's self-conjugacy defect is O(20): neither sector is self-conjugate). The realness lives *across* the two sectors, so inside the R-even block (where the scout works) a real-axis collision is the generic pseudo-Hermitian **defective** EP (PT-breaking). That is exactly the loud defective EP even N shows on the real axis.
- **At odd N** the reflection-fixed central site (i = (N−1)/2, an integer only at odd N) makes dim(R-even) − dim(R-odd) = (N−1)/2 ≠ 0 (3 at N=7, 4 at N=9; 0 at even N=6, 8). The dimension mismatch *forbids* the antiunitarity from pairing the sectors across, forcing it to act within each: each sector becomes **self-conjugate** (self-conjugacy defect ~10⁻¹⁴). The R-even block then carries genuine **real** eigenvalues, and two of them can cross at real q as a **semisimple (diabolic)** coalescence. The diabolic eigenvectors confirm the tie to the centre: they carry ~0.18 to 0.20 of their weight on the central-SE-site states (~0.05 to 0.06 on the reflection-fixed singletons).

So the realness and the parity are grounded in one stroke: the same dimension mismatch the fixed central site creates is what lets a real eigenvalue exist inside R-even and cross there. The fixed-site object is the reflection-fixed Jordan-Wigner (JW) band-centre zero mode (the free-fermion single-particle mode k = (N+1)/2, R-self-fixed, an integer mode only at odd N), the same structure [SLOW_MODE_R_PARITY](SLOW_MODE_R_PARITY.md) found splits a *different* coherence block's exceptional point at odd N. This is genuinely distinct from the N=4 self-fold (the antiunitary half-filling T = P·K, N=4-only, which fixes Re λ = −4): the odd-N diabolics sit at real λ but *not* at −4 or −N, because they are placed by the sector-self-conjugacy, not by T.

**The within-odd threshold (grounded on the residual; its onset is a density effect with two geometries).** The mechanism *permits* real-q diabolics at every odd N (R-even is self-conjugate at N=5 too), yet odd N=5 shows none. ## The cross-fold: the N=4 self-fold becomes a cross-block mirror, and the diabolics pair across it

The synthesis above leaves a loose end: the N=4 **self-fold** (the within-block antiunitary mirror that put one diabolic on the real axis) is N=4-only, because at N=4 the DE sector is its own Hamming complement. So where does it *go* for N ≥ 5? It does not vanish: it becomes a **cross-block** mirror.

The carrier is the branch-locus palindrome's bra bit-flip (the F89c structural lemma, [F89_TOPOLOGY_ORBIT_CLOSURE.md](F89_TOPOLOGY_ORBIT_CLOSURE.md)): the column flip ρ[a,b] → ρ[a,b̄] sends n_diff(a,b) ↦ n_diff(a,b̄) = N − n_diff(a,b), so it maps the (SE,DE) = (w1,w2) coherence block to the **(SE, w_{N−2}) = (w1, N−2)** block (the ket weight w1 is untouched, the bra weight w2 → N−2). At N=4, w_{N−2} = w2 = DE, so the partner *is* the (SE,DE) block: the N=4 self-fold is the degenerate **partner = self** case. For N ≥ 5 the partner is a different block: (SE,TE)=(w1,w3) at N=5, (w1,w4) at N=6, and so on.

**The fold is an EXACT antiunitary similarity at the matrix level**, not just a spectrum coincidence. Writing the bra-complement basis permutation as P (the index map |a⟩⟨b| ↦ |a⟩⟨b̄|), the two blocks satisfy

> L(1, N−2)(q̄) = −P · conj(L(1,2)(q)) · Pᵀ − 2N·I

to **machine zero** (the block entries are exact arithmetic; the residual is 0 for N=4..9 at every q, real *and* complex, the partner evaluated at the conjugate coupling q̄ per the F1 palindrome T·L(q)·T⁻¹ = −L(q̄) − 2σ). This is strictly stronger than the spectrum match the CLI's `foldcross` command reports: an antiunitary similarity preserves the entire **Jordan structure**, not merely the eigenvalues. Concatenating its three steps (complex conjugation, the similarity P·Pᵀ, and the affine −(·) − 2N) each preserves the dimensions of generalized eigenspaces, so a **semisimple** coalescence (a diabolic) maps to a semisimple coalescence.

**Move 4, answered.** Every (SE,DE) diabolic at (q, λ) therefore has a partner diabolic at (q̄, −λ̄ − 2N) in the (SE, w_{N−2}) block, with **identical character and identical coalescence gap**, for all N and all q at once, with no enumeration. The real-q diabolics (real λ, real q, so q̄ = q) pair at the *same* real q: at N=7 the q = 1.1264 diabolic (λ = −4.942, full-block coalescence gap 4.19·10⁻⁵) has its cross-fold partner in the (1,5) block at λ = −λ − 2N = −9.058, with the *same* gap 4.19·10⁻⁵. The complex-q diabolics pair at the **conjugate** coupling q̄. The N=4 "zeros" (the on-line self-mirror strands) become these cross-block mirror partners for N ≥ 5. The persistent evidence is a live C# witness, [`CrossFoldSimilarityWitness`](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Diagnostics/Foundation/CrossFoldSimilarityWitness.cs) (`inspect --root crossfold`): it builds both blocks from the shared [`WeightCoherenceBlock`](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Core/F89PathK/WeightCoherenceBlock.cs) and the bra-complement permutation, checks the similarity residual (machine zero, N=4..9), and reproduces the N=7 real-q pairing, all TDD-gated. Registered as **F89d** in [ANALYTICAL_FORMULAS](../docs/ANALYTICAL_FORMULAS.md) (the cross-block antiunitary similarity, a structural extension of the F89c Hamming-complement lemma) and typed as `F89CrossFoldSimilarityClaim` (parents F1 + the branch-locus palindrome).

### The cross-fold survives XXZ anisotropy: the pairing is integrability-independent

The Δ-test (Move 2) showed the diabolics *themselves* are integrability-protected: turn on the ZZ anisotropy Δ and they flip defective or lift. So the natural follow-on question is whether the **pairing** survives Δ, or dies with the diabolics. The handover guessed it might break: at Δ ≠ 0 the absorption-theorem rate flips under the bra-complement (n_diff → N − n_diff, the source of the −2N shift) while the ZZ-bond sum is invariant, and that asymmetry looked like it could spoil the clean carry.

It does not. The same antiunitary similarity holds for the **full interacting XXZ block** at *every* Δ,

> L(1, N−2)(q̄, Δ) = −P · conj(L(1,2)(q, Δ)) · Pᵀ − 2N·I

to machine zero for N = 4..9 at every q (real and complex) and every Δ. The Δ·ZZ term is the only addition over the XY block: a diagonal frequency −i·q·Δ·(zz(ket) − zz(bra)) with zz(c) = Σ_bond ⟨c|Z_bZ_{b+1}|c⟩. And zz is **even** under the global bit-flip the bra-complement performs, because each Z flips sign and the bond product Z_bZ_{b+1} ↦ (−Z_b)(−Z_{b+1}) = Z_bZ_{b+1} is unchanged, so zz(b̄) = zz(b). The bra-complement carries the ZZ term across the fold unchanged, exactly as it carries the XY hopping. The very invariance the handover flagged as a *worry* is what makes the fold *survive*.

So the two facts sit cleanly apart: under Δ the diabolics turn defective, but a diabolic and its cross-fold partner turn defective **in lockstep**; the two blocks stay antiunitary-similar at every Δ, the whole Jordan structure mirrored at each step of the dissolution. The cross-fold is therefore a **structural/algebraic** property of the Z-dephasing Liouvillian, not a free-fermion (integrability) artifact.

The discriminant is **bit-flip parity**. A bit-flip-*odd* perturbation breaks the fold: a longitudinal Z-field Σ_k w_k Z_k has fieldEnergy(b̄) = −fieldEnergy(b) (each Z_k flips sign), so its diagonal frequency is *not* preserved by the bra-complement and the residual jumps to O(1) (≈ 5.46 at N=6, verified as the negative control). The fold survives any bit-flip-even bond coupling (the ZZ anisotropy, longer-range ZZ) and breaks only under a bit-flip-odd one (a longitudinal field). Live evidence: the Δ=0 and Δ=0.7 residuals plus the field control are children of `inspect --root crossfold`; the Core gate is the `Build(n, wKet, wBra, q, Δ)` overload on [`WeightCoherenceBlock`](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Core/F89PathK/WeightCoherenceBlock.cs), pinned by `WeightCoherenceBlockTests` (`RCPsiSquared.Core.Tests`).

### What it docks onto: F89d is the F1 palindrome's bra leg, per block

The cross-fold is not a standalone curiosity.
