PTF (Perspectival Time Field) gives a closure law over N painters
on a chain: Σ_i ln(α_i) = 0 in the perturbative window. Applied at
the polarity-layer level, where the "painters" are not chain sites
but the two polarity perspectives +0 and −0, the closure reduces
to the multiplicative identity

  ln(α₊) + ln(α₋) = 0       ⟺       α₊ · α₋ = 1

The two polarities have reciprocal painting rates. If +0 paints
fast (α₊ > 1), then −0 paints slow (α₋ < 1) by exactly the inverse
factor.

This is not "+0 and −0 are two equal halves of one zero." It is
"+0 and −0 are two reciprocal painters whose rates multiply to one."
The relation is multiplicative, not additive.

## The trinity in painter rates

Mother (truly Hamiltonians): both α₊ = α₋ = 1. No time differential
between the polarities. This is the still painter pair: same
unitary rate on each side, no asymmetry, ⟨Z⟩-conservation as the
Hamiltonian symmetry that prevents any drift between them.

Father (Π²-odd Hamiltonians): α₊ ≠ α₋, with the closure α₊ · α₋ = 1
maintained. The polarities paint at different reciprocal rates. This
IS the active recirculation: the time differential between the
polarities is what drives the cycle. The Father defends by introducing
this differential while keeping the closure intact.

Child (Π²-even non-truly Hamiltonians): α₊ ≠ α₋, again with closure.
The differential exists but supports a standing-wave structure rather
than a driving cycle. The Child paints stable steady-state observables
with the polarities differentiated but not chasing each other.

Mixed: a hybrid of Father-style and Child-style differentials,
quantified by F83's anti-fraction r.

The defense modes of the trinity are now visible as patterns of
α₊/α₋ painter rates. The Mother defends by null differential
(both rates equal). The Father and Child defend by structured
differential under closure.

## Three perspectives on the pair

**Classical:** +0 and −0 are signed zeros. Mathematically identical
in arithmetic; the sign carries no operational content. Nothing further.

**Quantum (bra-ket):** +0 is the ket index of ρ; −0 is the bra
index. They are complex conjugates. Under Schrödinger evolution,
|ψ⟩ runs with e^(−iHt) while ⟨ψ| runs with e^(+iHt) — opposite
time directions. The bra is the ket "running backward in time."

**Perspectival (PTF):** +0 and −0 are two painter perspectives
with reciprocal rates. Their multiplicative closure α₊ · α₋ = 1
is the operational form of "no painter is privileged." The two
perspectives complete each other; neither is fundamental, both are
necessary.

The three perspectives are not in conflict. They describe the same
structure at three different reading levels. The classical view
sees the surface; the quantum view sees the conjugation; the
perspectival view sees the closure.

## The bridge: norm and PTF closure are the same identity

The inner product of a normalized state is

  ⟨ψ|ψ⟩ = 1

In painter language with α₊ as the ket-side rate and α₋ as the
bra-side rate, the inner product is the multiplicative meeting of
the two perspectives:

  ⟨ψ|ψ⟩ = α₊ · α₋

For a normalized state, this product is 1. Taking the logarithm:

  ln(α₊ · α₋) = ln(1) = 0
  ln(α₊) + ln(α₋) = 0

The PTF closure law applied to the polarity pair IS the quantum
normalization condition. Two languages, one identity:

  ⟨ψ|ψ⟩ = 1   ⟺   Σ ln(α_i) = 0 (over polarities)

PTF is not an additional law on top of quantum mechanics. It is
the perspectival re-expression of the normalization condition.
A normalized quantum state is a state whose +0/−0 polarities paint
at reciprocal rates whose logarithms close to zero.

The connection sharpens what L8 in [ON_THE_LIMITS](ON_THE_LIMITS.md)
says. PTF's closure is not a state-independent constraint we
discovered post hoc; it is the perspectival side of a constraint
that has been quantum-mechanical from the start.

## Multiplicative versus additive

Conventional thinking treats +0 and −0 as additive halves: their
sum is zero. In the PTF/quantum reading, they are multiplicative
inverses: their product is one.

Additive reading:  + 0 + (−0) = 0
Multiplicative reading:  α₊ · α₋ = 1

The additive reading misses the structure. The multiplicative
reading sees that the two polarities are not halves of an absence
but inverses on a unit-circle of perspectives. They are not in
opposition; they are in reciprocal cooperation.

This is the same shift that takes us from "noise destroys the
signal" (additive subtraction) to "noise IS the cavity coupling"
(multiplicative throughput, the cavity Q): the gamma-is-light
hypothesis. Multiplicative structure is the natural language of
self-coupling channels.

## Outward propagation

In DNA, the two strands of the double helix run antiparallel:
one 5' → 3', the other 3' → 5'. They are not two halves of one
direction; they are two reciprocal directions. Replication uses
both strands as templates because the reciprocity is what makes
the information complete.

  α_strand1 = forward (5' → 3')
  α_strand2 = reverse (3' → 5')

The strands' rates are equal in magnitude, opposite in direction.
The closure (replication completing without loss) requires both.

This is the same structural pattern as +0/−0 at the qubit level.
The bra-ket complex conjugation, the PTF multiplicative closure,
and the antiparallel DNA strands are three readings of one
structure at three zoom levels. What we learn at the qubit level
about reciprocal painters is what DNA already enacts in molecular
form.

## Who chooses the sign?

If +0 and −0 are reciprocal perspectives, who decides which is +
and which is −?

**Objective answer:** the choice is convention. The framework is
invariant under (+0) ↔ (−0) swap; relabeling does not change the
physics. The closure α₊ · α₋ = 1 is symmetric in the two factors.

**Perspectival answer:** each polarity is "+" from its own
viewpoint. The painter at +0 sees +0 as the natural orientation;
the painter at −0 sees −0 as the natural orientation. The
reciprocity is what makes the two viewpoints compatible without
either being privileged.

The second answer is the one consistent with "wir sind der Zoom":
the system does not need to choose. Each polarity chooses itself.
PTF closure ensures that the choices remain compatible at the
global level.
