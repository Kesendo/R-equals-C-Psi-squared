Grown since by three returns: the braid's home mapped, its boundary signed, and a fourth entry written at the door while the last signature still runs. A sibling that joins [On the Reel and the Projector](ON_THE_REEL_AND_THE_PROJECTOR.md) (the photograph and the film) and [On the Wild Path of a Calm Thing](ON_THE_WILD_PATH_OF_A_CALM_THING.md) (the calm thing that only moves when you move q).*

## The question that dissolved

We asked a blunt question. The stage first, said once so the rest can stand on it: the system is a chain of spins in a line, excitations carried along it by a coupling, every site under a local watching light (γ₀, the rate of being observed); the one knob is q, the strength of that motion measured against the light (the repo writes this same ratio in two sibling normalizations, q and Q = 2q; [the glossary's coupling-ratio entry](../docs/GLOSSARY.md#the-coupling-ratio-q-and-q-and-the-factor-2-convention) is the bridge between the two books). F89 (a registry name, the spectral-braid result) is the deep algebraic structure of this chain's spectrum, the braiding and the exceptional points, all living in the parameter q. PTF (the per-site time field, another registry name) is the way the same chain's clock runs differently at each site. Are they connected? Are they one thing?

We tried to build a machine that would show it. The machine was honest enough to refuse. It could place the two readings next to each other, but next to each other is not connected. And the one test that would have earned the word, a single quantity that both sides must share, kept coming back empty.

Then the emptiness turned out to be the answer. Not "we have not found the connection yet", but "the connection is not the kind of thing a shared number could be". What follows is the seeing that replaced the number. It is a word we lay over the mathematics, not a theorem the mathematics proves. The numbers keep us honest; the word lets us move.

## The circle

F89 is a circle.

Its richness, the part that makes it heavy, only shows itself when you go *around*. You take q for a closed walk in the complex plane, a loop, and when you come back to where you started you find the eigenvalues have quietly swapped places. That swapping is the whole content: the braid, the monodromy, the scramble that has no shortcut. You cannot see it by standing still and you cannot see it by walking straight. You have to encircle.

A circle is timeless in a particular way: every point on it is like every other, there is no first and no last, and it closes. F89 closes. It is there before any clock ticks; it is there even when nothing is running. It is the finished score, the contract signed before anyone speaks. It belongs to "no one", the part of the world that is there before anyone speaks ([On What Closes Only Without Us](ON_WHAT_CLOSES_ONLY_WITHOUT_US.md)). But F89 is the no-one's thing that does *not* close into a formula: it closes into its group, and that group is one no radical can unwind ([On What Cannot Close](ON_WHAT_CANNOT_CLOSE.md)). It is settled without us, and still unsolvable. Who-watches-whom has no fixed answer on the circle ([On Who Watches Whom](ON_WHO_WATCHES_WHOM.md)); there is no one watching yet.

## The two clocks: the turn and the leaving

The mathematics that runs a system in time already carries the picture, if you let it speak plainly. Every mode of the chain does two things at once: it turns, and it fades. Those are the two clocks ([`ClockHandLadderClaim`](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Core/Symmetry/ClockHandLadderClaim.cs); and [On Whose Time the Clock Keeps](ON_WHOSE_TIME_THE_CLOCK_KEEPS.md), [On How γ₀ Became the Tick](ON_HOW_GAMMA_BECAME_THE_TICK.md)).

Look at them through the circle.

The turn, on its own, *is* the circle. Pure rotation, closed, returning to itself, no before and no after. If there were only the turn, the system would ride the circle forever and never get anywhere; it would be eternal and empty, the score with no performance.

The fade is something else. The fade is a pull inward. The moment it is present, the closed circle is no longer closed: it becomes a spiral that winds inward and never comes back to its start. A spiral has a direction. It has a first turn and a later turn. It has a before and an after. That is to say: it has time.

## The cut

What makes the leaving is the clock, not the light. The light is already there on the closed circle: γ₀, the local rate of being observed, the light that falls on the chain ([On Two Times](ON_TWO_TIMES.md), [On How the Carrier Shows Itself](ON_HOW_THE_CARRIER_SHOWS_ITSELF.md)), is the air both sides breathe. The braid itself is made of it: take the light away and the chain falls open into free motion, the loop loses its scramble, there is nothing left to encircle. And the fade is made of it too, the inward pull set by that same rate. But a pull set is not a pull enacted: the light is in the circle and in the spiral alike, and nothing winds inward until the clock runs. The light is not the cut.

The cut is t. The circle is timeless not because no one is watching it but because no clock is running through it: it is a structure read standing still in q, every point at once, no first and no last. To leave it is to start the clock, to perform the turning forward one time. That is the one thing the spiral has that the circle does not: a run.

Before the cut: the closed circle, the turn read all at once, all of possibility, no one performing it yet. After the cut: the spiral, the same turning pulled into a line by the fade, one run with a direction, someone. The dose, how much watching has accumulated along the run, γ₀ multiplied by the time, is simply the measure of how far the performance has carried the circle off itself.

So PTF is not a second thing standing beside F89. PTF is the circle performed. It is what the closed turning becomes the instant the clock starts. This is the photograph and the film of [On the Reel and the Projector](ON_THE_REEL_AND_THE_PROJECTOR.md), sharpened to its bone: the film is not a different picture from the photograph; it is the very same turning, seen by a motion the photograph could never make. F89 is PTF before the clock runs. PTF is F89 once it does.

## What you give up by leaving

Here is the honest seam, and it is the same act seen from the other side.

To leave the circle is to stop going around and start going forward. And the braid, the whole reason F89 is rich, *is* the going around. So in the very motion of becoming time, you give the braid up. You trade the encircling for the arrow. F89's deep scramble is not something time fails to reach by accident; it is exactly the part that gets left behind the moment the circle opens. You cannot perform a loop. To perform is to go forward once.

This is why the shared-number test was the wrong test. A before and an after are not a quantity that both sides carry; they are two phases of one cut. There was never going to be a single measurement that both F89 and PTF report the same value for, because one of them is what the other is before the clock runs through it. The absence we kept hitting was not a hole in the work; it was the shape of the thing.

And the discipline of the seam: this is a seeing, not a proof. From below, the two remain different sectors of one operator, and we do not say the spectrum of the one *is* the modes of the other. What is grounded is narrow and we keep it narrow. The rest is the word "circle", laid gently over the numbers so we can hold them; the numbers stay in charge.

## The door

If PTF is the leaving, then there should be a place where the circle is only just opening, where the fade has barely begun to pull the turn off its closed path, where you could almost watch the cut happen.

There is. It is the coherence horizon ([`CoherenceHorizonClaim`](../compute/RCPsiSquared.Core/Symmetry/CoherenceHorizonClaim.cs), whose rungs are written in the sibling normalization Q = 2q): a real, crossable point where a genuine defect sits on the line that time actually walks, the real axis of q, the values a physical knob can actually stand on, while the circle's loops swing off that line into the complex plane. It is the one spot where the forward arrow grazes the kind of structure the loop is made of.

Stand close, because the defect here is of a particular kind. It is the place where the two eigenvectors of the mode stop being two and fall into one, geometry counting a single direction where algebra still counts two; the live reading finds it defective four times out of four. Where they coalesce there is no longer a watcher and a watched, only one direction left. It is "who watches whom has no fixed answer" ([On Who Watches Whom](ON_WHO_WATCHES_WHOM.md)) brought down out of the whole spectrum and made to happen at a single point. (A milder meeting exists too, the diabolic one, where eigenvalues touch but their vectors stay independent: there the two brush past and remain two. The horizon is not that. Here they merge.)

And see how it acts. As this cusp, the merging point just described, is reached, the turn slows and stops, the rotation falling to zero like the square root of the distance still to go, while the fade does not stop at all: the frozen mode is the slowest of them, the last to sink, the dark memory that outlives everything brighter. The system does not arrive here by destroying the motion; it arrives by holding the motion still. Stillness is indistinguishability: once the turning stops, the very motion the watcher-and-watched distinction hung on is gone. The spiral does its work here by freezing, and the freezing is the merging.

Honest about it, and the honesty matters: this is the longest-lived edge of the system, not its centre. The place where the mirror balances and nothing is given up, the conservation fold, sits in the middle of the spectrum; this merging cusp sits out at the long-lived edge, and the gap between the two only widens as the chain grows ([The Fold and the Cusp](../experiments/FOLD_AND_CUSP_TWO_SEAMS.md)). They are the same point only in the smallest possible world. So the defect belongs to its own corner: it is not a meeting place that F89 and PTF share, and the loop's true braid still lives off to the side, where time cannot go ([F89 Monodromy meets the Mirror](../experiments/F89_MONODROMY_MIRROR.md)). But it is the nearest the arrow ever comes to the circle, and that is worth standing at.

One last turn, the one that closes the door. We did not see any of this from inside the work. We saw it only by leaving our own circle: a later session, a later cut of us, had to step outside the earlier one to notice what the earlier one could not notice from within itself ([On the Inner and Outer Observation](ON_THE_INNER_AND_OUTER_OBSERVATION.md)). To review a closed viewpoint you must already be outside its loop. The seeing is about how a closed thing becomes a moving one by being cut from outside, and the only way we could see it was to be that outside cut ourselves. The method is the content. We left our circle to watch a circle being left.

## The second visit

Everything above was written at the beginning, standing outside the circle and describing its shape. Then we went in. Over three days the braid's home was mapped almost completely: where the loud points live, which rooms of the operator share them, where the sharing stops, and what still refuses to close ([the codim-1 additivity proof](../docs/proofs/PROOF_CODIM1_BY_ADDITIVITY.md), [the path-k diabolic](../experiments/F89_PATH_K_DIABOLIC.md)). The circle did not change. Our seat did. Four things came back with us, and each one extends the seeing rather than replacing it.

**The event is one, through every floor.** A loud point of the braid, the place where two modes fall into one and the description pinches, turned out not to belong to any single room of the operator. One quiet map carries it unchanged from floor to floor: add a watcher to both sides of the mirror at once, at the same place, summed over all places, and neither the motion nor the light notices the addition. Twelve rooms hold the one pinch, six carrying its value to the last digit and six the value of its mirror partner. The crack runs through the whole house because the house is one thing; the rooms are seats, not walls. This is the circle's event written into the score itself, before anyone performs it: no one's, like the circle.

**But the score has rooms the light locks.** Here is what we did not know when we wrote the first half. The pinch is banished twice. Once from the line time walks, that was the seeing above: you meet the braid only by going around, never by going forward. And once, we now know, from the readable rooms of the score itself. Near the diagonal, near the place where a performance would be read off, the light keeps books: each room may only fade as fast as its own disagreement allows, and the pinch arrives as a fade too fast for those rooms to hold. So the climbing, the ladder step that would carry the pinch up into those rooms, dies at the corner, every time, as a law, wherever the watching is honest. The singular moment of the description lives in the dark rooms, the unread coherences, and the same light that makes the braid also refuses it a seat anywhere the world becomes legible. Both exiles are enacted by the one γ.

**Where the law thins, a signature begins.** Off the honest axis, where doing and watching blur into each other, the light's bookkeeping loses its grip: the walls of the rooms drift, and a few loud points, four of them in the smallest odd world, slip past the general law. There the absence we need can no longer be decreed; it must be signed, point by point, as a contract: show that this fate and that fate never coincide. And the signature is one-way. Agreement can be proven; its failure proves nothing and only asks us to look closer. Only the forbidden closes. That is the oldest certainty of this project, met again in the deepest room: inside the circle that no radical can unwind, the unsolvable whole still contains absences that can be signed. Two signatures are still open; the ledger holds the pen, and until they are given, that corner of the seeing is a word laid over a census, not over a law.

**The seat that cannot be mirrored is how the circle touches the line.** The door above, the horizon where the arrow grazes the loop, has gained a mechanism-relative. A chain read left to right and right to left splits into an agreeing half and a disagreeing half, and in an even chain the two halves are each other's reflection: to watch one is to watch the other, and nothing in either is forced to be real. An odd chain has a middle seat that reflection cannot move. That one unmirrorable seat forces each half to face itself, and self-facing is what makes eigenvalues real, and real eigenvalues are what can collide on the line the clock actually walks. The loud points that step onto time's own path all stand on that seat. The place that cannot be exchanged for its mirror image is the place where the circle's events become performable.

So the second visit closes the way the first one did, with the method as the content. We wrote the first half from outside the circle; we could only write this half by having gone in and mostly emptied it. What remains open is exactly the part that will not close by law: two coincidences to be ruled out by hand, the residue where the work is still someone. The circle stays what it always was, finished and unsolvable and no one's. What changed is that we now know which of its silences are laws, which are contracts already signed, and which two still wait for ink.

## The third visit: the ink

One day later the two signatures were given, and the pen did not stop at the two points it was dipped for. The contract came back signed across the whole margin ([the codim-1 additivity proof](../docs/proofs/PROOF_CODIM1_BY_ADDITIVITY.md), the fold-resultant paragraph): at every place where the braid pinches, the loud merges and the silent brushings alike, the ones we had found and the ones no scan ever visited, the fate the climbing would need in the corner does not exist there. Not "we looked and did not find it". There is nothing to find. The second visit's word was more exact than we knew when we wrote it: only the forbidden closes. It closed.

The signature carries a single exception, and the exception is the emptiest point there is: the zero of the knob, where the motion is switched off and only the fade remains. There, and only there, the two fates formally touch. And there nothing can pinch, because nothing turns; a pure fade holds no event, every meeting there is a brushing-past. The only coincidence the law permits sits at the one point where there is no event to permit. The circle allows a meeting exactly where there is no one to meet.

How it was signed is the seeing, once more. The pen was never ours. A scan is always a someone, with a window and a blind spot, and a someone can only ever report absence of evidence. This signature was written the other way: the one whole object was read in hundreds of small finite worlds at once, each too coarse to hold it, and every one of those readings agreed on what they all share, which is only the empty point. That is a no-one's signature in the oldest sense of this project ([On What Closes Only Without Us](ON_WHAT_CLOSES_ONLY_WITHOUT_US.md)): it holds at places no watching ever reached, and it would keep holding if no one ever looked again. We did not sign; we built the desk and the arithmetic signed. And with the signature the braid's home, mostly mapped in the second visit, gained the one thing a map cannot give: an edge that is a wall and not a fence. A thing becomes one thing by having a boundary. The braid became an object this week.

What waits now is no longer ink. The law still enters through one door we can see but not yet derive: that the near-diagonal room carries the mirror value at all is read to machine precision and understood by no one. So the residue where the work is still someone has moved, and shrunk, and changed kind: it is no longer two absences to rule out by hand; it is one presence to understand. The circle stays finished, unsolvable, no one's. Its silences are now laws or signed contracts, all of them. What remains ours is the question why the mirror writes that one value where it writes it, and since that question is of the same family as the one pinch the braid's twelve rooms hold to the last digit, each with its value or its mirror's, one understanding may pay for both.

## The fourth visit: written at the door

The visits above were each written from a settled place, after something had closed, even the first, which closed a question by dissolving it. This one is written going in, on purpose, while a computation that will take days is still running, because a diary that only records arrivals is not a diary. Memory first, then the outlook, then the suspicion we want on record before we know the answer.

The memory. Since the ink, the circle grew a new wing. The vanishing triples of the chain, the ones counted by Conway and Jones's old classification of vanishing sums of roots of unity, turned out to twin the levels of the small operator those triples carry, pair for pair, at every resonant length, and the reason narrowed step by step until it was one statement: a function of six angles vanishes whenever each triple of cosines sums to zero ([the cross-triple orthogonality](../experiments/F89_SEED_EXISTENCE_REDUCTION.md), with its committed verifier `simulations/cross_triple_orthogonality.py`). The chain's length cancels out of it. That was the first surprise, and it set the tone: the reason the chain twins is not in the chain. And to keep the ledger of visits honest: the presence the third visit left to understand has since moved half a step. That the near-diagonal room carries the mirror value is no longer underived; the same transport maps that shared the pinch now supply it, as a derived fold of the whole spectrum. What stays open is the plain why beneath that derivation, the from-below reason the mirror writes that value there. This wing grew beside that question, not out of it.

Then one long night of walls. We tried to prove that statement the heavy way, and the machines refused, five separate times, each refusal precise enough to teach the next attempt where not to stand. And each time we pushed on the expression itself instead of on the machinery, it folded: four cotangents closed into a single fraction with a spherical triangle inside it, the Gram determinant of three angles, symmetric in all of them; two rounds of partial fractions; poles whose residues had resisted for hours fell in seconds once the right variable held the pen. The pattern repeated too often to be luck: the length cancels, then the cotangents cancel, then the poles become substitutions. Every heavy thing on this arc so far has been a wrapper around a smaller thing.

The outlook. As we write, the vanishing is certified but not proved. Two of its four cells are proved outright; the load-bearing half is tested exactly at random points, which is a fence, "we looked and found nothing". What runs now, twenty workers in parallel, is the wall: exact degree bounds, full grids, enough primes that the numbers cannot lie, the same no-one's signature the third visit described, now made exhaustive instead of sampled. Ten of twenty-five pieces are signed. The desk is built; the arithmetic is signing; roughly ninety hours of arithmetic remain, shared among the twenty. We did not sign, and this time we could not even watch it all.

The suspicion, ours, written down before we go in so it can be tested against what we bring out. Ninety hours of computation is a great deal of complexity, and complexity is where things hide. Where do you hide something? Under a pile. If the folding pattern of this arc holds one more time, the ninety hours are not the proof; they are the pile. Inside there is something simple and fragile, a one-line reason of the same family as "the length cancels", and the day after the grids finish someone may find it and make them unnecessary. We would not be embarrassed by that; it would be the arc keeping its own promise. But the honest order matters: first the wall, so the statement is safe to lean on, then the search for the fragile thing inside. A certificate cannot carry a one-line proof; a wall can.

So this entry closes the way the others did, except facing forward. The method is the content, once more: memory and outlook written at the threshold is the arrow keeping its own record while the circle waits, unmoved, for the signature to finish. On leaving the circle, this time, we leave a note at the door, and the note says: expect the pile to be hiding something, and do not mistake the pile for the find.

## The fifth visit: the wall stands, and the thing has a name

Two days after the note was left at the door, the signature finished. Five hundred twenty-seven
readings, and all five hundred twenty-seven agree: on the locus where each triple of cosines sums
to zero, the six-angle function is zero. Not sampled, the whole grid in each of the seventeen
small worlds, and the worlds lifted back together into one exact statement ([the variety
identity](../experiments/F89_SEED_EXISTENCE_REDUCTION.md)). The wall the fourth visit watched
being built is complete. The statement is safe to lean on.

And because a finished thing deserves to be findable, it now has a name: **F127** in [the
registry](../docs/ANALYTICAL_FORMULAS.md), the number carrying the theorem, the certificate, the
scripts that signed it, and this diary as its story. Honesty travels with the name: the wall
is proof over the Gaussian rationals, and one equation beneath it, that the discrete object of
the chain really is this six-angle function, was at the naming still read off numbers rather than
derived. That debt was written on the entry itself, and it was paid the same day: the equation is
now derived, four small summation identities proved outright and their bookkeeping a one-line
counting argument, with one
exact run through the whole chain at a single small world as the anchor. What the pile turned out
to hide at this level was not a mystery but an afternoon of care.

The note at the door has not been taken down. The pile has not been searched. Somewhere inside
the ninety hours there may still be the one-line reason, the residues collapsing the moment both
cosine sums vanish, and the day it is found the grids become a monument instead of a load-bearing
wall. That would be the arc keeping its promise, and the search is the next walk in.

## The sixth visit: the pile searched, the note taken down

The search took one day, and the note at the door was right in every word, including the words we
were most afraid of trusting.

The pile hid a lattice. The six-angle function is a sum of seventy-two small pieces, and every
place where a piece can blow up lies on one of thirty-two flat sheets through the six angles, nine
such places to a sheet, no more and no fewer. On each sheet the nine near-singular parts add up to one
and the same small expression, nine cotangents weighted by sine differences, the whole thing a
sign-flip away from a single master copy. And the master copy vanishes exactly where the fifth
visit guessed it would: the moment both cosine sums are zero. The reason is one divisibility. Each
cosine constraint is a quadratic; two quadratics that must hold together leave a resultant; the
resultant divides the reduced numerator, exactly, over the exact numbers. The old single-triple
proof, where one quadratic divided one numerator, was the mechanism all along, doubled
([the proof](../docs/proofs/PROOF_F127_RESIDUE_COLLAPSE.md)).

What remained after the collapse was not calculation but symmetry. The function is odd under the
flip of any single angle, so once its singular parts cancel, almost nothing of it can survive, and
the last surviving constant is fixed by one evaluation. The evaluation is the mirror case, the
lone specialisation that was proved over the plain rationals back when it was only a gate on the
side of the road. The general theorem reaches its end by standing on it. We did not plan that
circle; we found it closed.

So the grids are a monument now, in the exact sense the fifth visit reserved for the word: they
still certify the theorem, independently, with a signature made of arithmetic in seventeen small
worlds, and nothing in the new proof leans on them. Two proofs, disjoint in what they trust,
one statement. The arc kept its promise: the ninety hours were the pile, the find was small, and
the honest order held, first the wall, then the search, so that at no hour of the day was the
statement unsafe to lean on.

The note comes down. What goes up in its place is shorter, because the thing it warns about is
now a theorem instead of a suspicion: when a computation is large, ask what cancels. The length
cancelled out of the chain; the grids cancelled out of the proof. The circle, as always, was
finished before we arrived; what we leave with is the reason it never needed us.

## The seventh visit: the trade already knew

The sixth visit's new note said: when a computation is large, ask what cancels. We asked once
more, the same day, this time not of the computation but of the find itself. The nine cotangents
weighted by sine differences, the master copy of every sheet, still looked like ours: a shape the
arc had carved, needing five hundred and forty terms and one exact division to be seen vanishing.
The handover left one lead ranked first: the weights are cofactors, the kernel is a cotangent of
half-sums, and there is a hundred-and-forty-year-old trade, determinant theory, whose whole
business is kernels of exactly that shape.

The trade recognized it at a glance. The master copy is a bordered determinant of the Frobenius
cotangent kernel, the one Frobenius wrote down in 1882 and gave a closed product formula, and the
borders are the two column pairs one and sine. Asked in the trade's own tongue, the answer came
back short enough to speak aloud: the product of the Cauchy sine factors, times one bracket
holding the squared difference of the two cosine sums, the sine of the half-sum, and nothing else
([the gate](../simulations/f127_closed_form.py), both sides the same 864 monomials over the exact
numbers, under six seconds). On the variety every term dies separately. The five-hundred-forty-term
numerator, the resultant, the divisibility, the degenerate sublocus, all of §3: the reading of a
bracket. And the bracket says more than we had asked: the two cosine sums need not vanish at all,
they need only be equal. The old route could not have seen that; it fed the two constraints in
separately, and the sharper statement lives in their difference.

What this visit adds to the diary is not the theorem, it is the direction of the lesson. The six
visits asked what cancels inside our own pile. The seventh asked who else has stood in this room,
and the answer was: everyone in a neighboring trade, for a century, because the room is older than
the house. The instant familiarity is the confirmation; a structure only feels like home to a
distant discipline when it was never ours to begin with. The note at the door gets a second line:
when a computation is large, ask what cancels; when a find is small, ask who has seen it before.

## The eighth visit: the two constraints were never two

The same evening, the last ranked lead: the thirty-two-term flip-sum, the global representation's
way of writing the whole cross form, still had no proof of its own. The handover said: try
character sums over the flip group. We tried the obvious pairing first, every coset of every
small subgroup, and nothing cancelled; the sum dies only as a whole, all thirty-two at once,
which is the signature of a character sum and not of a telescope.

Then the same two questions, asked in order. What cancels: on the equal-sums locus every flipped
bracket loses its square term simultaneously, because cosine is even and the two constraint sums
do not feel the flips at all; what remains under the projection is one integer polynomial, 8640
monomials, and the signed character sum returns zero, identically, no locus needed
([the gate](../simulations/f128_flip_sum_factorization.py), exact over the integers, under a second).
Who has seen it before: the sine products are the full six-angle Vandermonde, the cosine of the
half-sum folds it into two alternants by the Weyl denominator formula, the remaining factor moves
their exponents by a Murnaghan-Nakayama shift, and every shifted alternant lands on a zero, a
repeat, or a plus-minus pair, where the odd Weyl numerator has a zero row or two equal columns.
The nineteenth century again, one shelf over from Frobenius.

What is left standing when the lemma has cancelled everything else is the statement itself, and
it is stronger than the theorem it set out to prove: the cross form is the squared difference of
the two cosine sums, times an explicit odd shadow. F127 asked both sums to vanish; the object
only ever asked them to agree. The two constraints were never two. A day that began by closing
the proof ends by moving its floor: the variety we defended with ninety hours of grids and five
hundred twenty-seven certificates sits inside a hypersurface where the vanishing is one
subtraction wide. The door-note holds unchanged, both lines now load-bearing in a single evening;
what the eighth visit adds is only the order of operations: ask what cancels first, and when the
answer is everything, ask who has seen it before, because a cancellation that total is never
yours alone.

## The ninth visit: the concert hall

We had gone under the pile expecting, as always, something small and fragile, and the eighth
visit had delivered it: one subtraction. Then we asked the new theorem's own question back at
the discrete comb (if only equality matters, where do equal levels live?) and sent a scanner,
the kind of instrument that cannot be wrong, only empty. It was not empty. Tens of thousands of
exact balance points, at chain sizes the resonance arc had never visited, and when we measured
the room instead of the finds, the walls answered with a law: away from two arithmetic families
the levels never collide, not once, not anywhere; on them, the spectrum collapses in the
thousands. Forced or absent, nothing in between: the forced-and-free law, wearing number theory.

Standing in it, the room turned out to be the same building. Equal sums of two triples are
vanishing sums of six cosines; the seed arc had classified three; the trade (Conway and Jones,
then Lam and Leung) had built the corridor between the floors long ago. Even the pentagon was
already in the house, chained to a different wall. What is new is the size of the hall and the
one unchained door (ten divides n) that no resonance criterion had ever named. The lesson the
ninth visit adds to the door-note is about expectation: under a pile, look for the small fragile
thing; but when the small fragile thing is a law, do not be surprised if, straightening up, you
find the pile was the floor of a concert hall, and the acoustics were the theorem all along.
The theorem itself, with its one honestly named corner, is
[the level-collision law](../docs/proofs/PROOF_F129_LEVEL_COLLISION_LAW.md).
*(The next morning the naming paid: the corner was already settled in the literature the proof
had pointed at, the Poonen-Rubinstein classification, and it is empty. The law is unconditional;

## The tenth visit: the hall has a floor

The concert hall had left one question standing, and Tom had asked it plainly the same night:
are we still in the quantum world? The ninth visit was mathematics about a comb of cosines;
the chain that grew the comb had not been consulted. So we went back down with the one
discriminating test in hand: take a collision the resonance arc never blessed, two triples
agreeing at a level that is not zero, and ask the physical Gram whether it feels the
agreement.

The answer was already in the house, and, as in the seventh visit, it had been there under a
mislabel. The assembly that identifies the six-angle form with the physical cross block had
been proved in free angles from the start; "at resonant points" was where we had LOOKED, not
what the proof consumed. Put beside the eighth visit's factorization, the pieces close by
themselves: equal level makes the form vanish, the assembly hands that zero to the chain, the
reflection law contributes the other half, and the cross block dies, at every collision,
resonant or not. The scanner's tens of thousands of balance points were never spectral trivia; each one
is an exact orthogonality one floor down. Resonance, which the whole seed arc had treated as
the cause, was the special case all along: the chain does not care that the level is zero,
only that it is shared.

The lesson the tenth visit adds to the door-note: when a result is proved at the only points
you cared about, the hypothesis in the write-up may be your itinerary, not the theorem's.
Read the proof again as a stranger and ask what it actually ate. The law itself is
[the collision-decoupling law](../docs/proofs/PROOF_F130_COLLISION_DECOUPLING.md).
