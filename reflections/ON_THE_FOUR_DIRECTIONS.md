# On the Four Directions

Where does a small quantum world keep the length of its life?

Not, it turns out, in time. Time is only where the answer gets read off. The length is kept in shape, and the shape stands in three other directions before the fourth, the time one, ever comes into it.

Start with the two the world moves in. A small chain's life is motion across its places: a shimmer that travels, back and forth, from one site to the next, never resting long in any of them. That traveling is a plane, the floor the whole dance happens on. Nothing about how long the world lasts is decided here. This is only where it moves.

Stand a third direction up from that floor and call it the one the [light falls from](../docs/THE_THREE_DIAGONALS.md). Along this direction the world is watched. The watching is far simpler than the dance it watches: at every place it asks a single question, how much are you at odds with yourself here, how much do you flicker between two ways of being instead of holding one, and it keeps only the [count](../docs/proofs/PROOF_ABSORPTION_THEOREM.md). That count, place by place, is the whole grip the light has. The more loudly a place flickers against itself, the more clearly the light reads it, and the faster it is worn away. So to last, a world must arrange itself to show this third direction as little as it can: lie low, lie even, lie quiet.

And here is the one that shows the least. Not the full chain and not the empty one, but the half, with half its places lit and half left dark, which can spread its shimmer thinnest and most evenly across them all: a single slow swell from one end to the other, the gentlest standing wave there is. Spread so thin and so even, it gives the watching almost nothing to read. That, and no number handed to it from outside, is its long life: the survivor, the incomplete, the last thing the light can find.

Now the fourth direction, the one we set out to find: how long it lasts. The thing worth saying, the thing that took us a while to see, is that this fourth length is not its own free quantity, kept somewhere off to the side. It is read off the other three, off the shape in the plane as the watching sees it.

You can test this by hand. Reach into the world at one place and change how strongly two of its sites are tied. The survivor's length of life shifts, but only by as much as its slow swell was rising at the place you touched. At the still ends, where the wave lies flat against the wall and goes nowhere, you can do nothing to its life at all. In the moving middle, where the swell climbs steepest, you change it most. Its length of life is written in the [slope of its own standing wave](../docs/proofs/PROOF_DIFFUSION_RAYLEIGH_CLOSURE.md), and nowhere else. Not luck, not a dial set from outside: the plain shape of the thing, read by the light, given back as a span of time.

So the four directions were never four of a kind. A world moves in two of them. It is watched along a third. And the fourth, how long it lasts, is not another direction at all, but those first two read back along the third: the shape it takes in its plane, counted by the light, returned as a length. It does not [survive in time](ON_TWO_TIMES.md). It survives by its shape, and time is only where that shape's verdict is finally read.

The incomplete spreads itself thinnest, and so the light can barely read it, and so it lasts longest. And the same slow, even wave is read once more in that last direction, not only how long it lasts but where, along its own rising, that length can still be moved: untouchable where it lies flat against the wall, alive to the touch where it climbs. One shape, and the time direction reads it twice.
