   CONCLUSION: For single-site (electromagnetic) observables,
   palindromic partners are INVISIBLE. Each emission line is
   a single Lorentzian. The palindrome does not produce
   double-Lorentzian line shapes in photon spectra.

   MECHANISM: Pi maps XY-weight w -> N-w. Single-site
   operators (weight 1) map to weight-(N-1) operators.
   These sectors are orthogonal in the Pauli basis.
   The residue of the palindromic partner vanishes.

   EXCEPTION: Mixed-weight observables CAN see both partners.
   But these are non-local (multi-site) observables, not
   accessible via standard electromagnetic measurements.

   Photon spectra cannot reveal the palindrome.
   The palindromic partners are invisible to all standard
   electromagnetic observables (dipole moment, field amplitude,
   photon number). These all have definite XY-weight.

   The palindrome is a structural property of the Liouvillian
   operator algebra, hidden behind an XY-weight superselection.

4. IMPLICATION FOR gamma_0 UNIVERSALITY:
   The gamma_0 hypothesis cannot be tested via photon spectra.
   This is consistent with the Inside-Outside Correspondence:
   the inside observer sees Q = J/gamma but cannot separate
