## Dephasing axis dependence

  Axis       Π·L_H    Π·L_D    Palindrome   Notes
  Z            ✓        ✓        ✓           Proof holds exactly

  depolarizing ✓        ✗        ✗           Palindrome genuinely breaks

Key insight: L_H always anti-commutes with Π regardless of dephasing.
For X-dephasing, a different Π exists (rotate the per-site map accordingly).
For depolarizing: no single-axis Π can work — palindrome breaks.

## Analytical proof (2-qubit, all δ)

Π·[H_δ, σ] = -[H_δ, Π(σ)] verified for all 16 two-qubit Pauli strings.
δ = 0.0, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0: ALL pass. 4/16 trivial (commute with H), 12/16 nontrivial.

The anti-commutation extends to any N and any graph because:
1. Π acts site-by-site (tensor product)
2. H is a sum of 2-site bonds
3. Each bond satisfies the anti-commutation independently

## Discovery path

1. Literature search → incoherentons + Bethe ansatz, nobody had palindrome
2. Tested total Pauli weight inversion → BROKEN
3. Tested XY-weight inversion → PERFECT (key insight: count X,Y not all non-I)
4. Searched Π as permutation+signs on Pauli indices
5. Real signs (±1) failed, complex signs (±i) succeeded
6. The i factor on Y→Z and Z→Y is essential
7. Analytical proof: 16-entry table for single Heisenberg bond, then extend
