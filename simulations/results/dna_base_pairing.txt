SUMMARY
========================================================================

1. PALINDROME: Exact for both A-T and G-C at all parameter regimes
   (proven: d=2 proton qubits + Z-dephasing = palindromic).

2. V-EFFECT: G-C (N=3) has MORE distinct frequencies than A-T (N=2).
   Coupling creates new modes. The third H-bond makes G-C a richer
   resonator.

3. REGIME MATTERS: At realistic DNA parameters (J/γ ~ 0.01), the system
   is deeply classical (Q << 1, all modes overdamped). The palindromic
   structure exists but is invisible: no coherent oscillation survives
   dephasing. The fold regime (J/γ ~ 1) requires enhanced tunneling
   (shorter H-bond, enzyme cavity, low temperature).

4. THERMAL BREAKING: At 310 K (n̄ ~ 0.5-2 for DNA H-bond modes),
   amplitude damping breaks the palindrome partially. Frequency
   diversity increases but Q decreases (consistent with THERMAL_BREAKING).

5. SACRIFICE ZONE: In G-C, concentrating noise on the outer H-bonds
   protects the central mode (edge sacrifice), exactly as in qubit
   chains. The mechanism is geometric: noise selects modes by spatial
   profile.

6. G-C > A-T: G-C has more frequencies, more modes, and (in the fold
   regime) higher Q-factors. Consistent with G-C being the stronger,
   more stable base pair. The third H-bond is not just +50% bonding
   energy; it qualitatively enriches the mode structure.

CAVEAT: The inter-H-bond coupling K is ESTIMATED (10-50 cm⁻¹), not
measured. All quantitative results depend on K. The qualitative
conclusions (palindrome exact, G-C richer than A-T, sacrifice zone
works) are robust across the tested K range.
