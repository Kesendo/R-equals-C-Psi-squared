Reading guide:
  α[i]            = time-rescaling factor s.t. P_A(α[i]·t) ≈ P_B(i, t)
                    α=1 → site evolves like baseline; α<1 → slower; α>1 → faster.
  Σ ln α          = PTF closure law (Tier 2). PTF predicts ≈ 0 in perturbative window.
  max RMSE        = worst per-site fit residual (mismatch with L_B not actually a
                    rescaling of L_A → fit is forced; large RMSE means PTF model
                    breaks for this case).

Observation: PTF was designed for SINGLE-BOND defects (small δJ on XY chain).
Forcing it onto wildly-different H (XY+YX, YZ+ZY, etc.) tests how far the
perspectival-time-field reading extends. Large RMSE = the case is outside the
perturbative regime PTF was tuned for; the α values are then 'best L_A-rescaling
approximation' rather than a structurally honest factorisation.
