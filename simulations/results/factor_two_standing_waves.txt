SUMMARY
===========================================================================

1. FACTOR 2: Mean absorption rate ratio (unpaired/paired) verified
   at every N = 2 through 7 and every topology (chain, star, ring).

2. STANDING WAVE STRUCTURE: Paired modes have matching frequencies
   and complementary absorption rates (Re(fast) + Re(slow) = -2Ng).

3. THE TWO MIRRORS: The palindromic symmetry operator Pi and the
   identity I are the two mirrors of the Fabry-Perot. Every paired
   mode bounces between them, halving its effective absorption.

4. TOPOLOGY-INDEPENDENT: The factor 2 holds for chain, star, and ring.
   The topology changes which modes exist, but not the factor by which
   standing waves reduce absorption.

5. BEER-LAMBERT: The absorption coefficient alpha = 2*gamma per site,
   independent of N. This is the fundamental absorption rate of the
   medium. Standing waves see half this rate because they travel
   through the medium twice (forward + backward).
