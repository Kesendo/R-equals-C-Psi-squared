===========================================================================
SUMMARY
===========================================================================

1. WILSON-COWAN EIGENFREQUENCY: At standard biological parameters,
   the E-I dynamics oscillates at ~12 Hz at LOW input (I_ext < 0.5)
   and becomes overdamped at higher input (sigmoid saturation).
   The oscillation is spontaneous, not input-driven -- like a laser
   above threshold, not like a passive cavity.

2. PARAMETER MAPPING: At I_ext = 0 (spontaneous oscillation),
   the mapping to cavity modes gives omega_WC ~ 12 Hz. The cavity
   formula requires J_eff and N_eff, but the overdamped biological
   operating point prevents a clean mapping.

3. C. ELEGANS: The connectome's linearized dynamics produces
   oscillating modes with a frequency distribution that can be
   compared to cavity mode structure.

4. ANESTHESIA: Below I_ext_crit, oscillations disappear. This IS
   'turning off the light.' The cavity goes dark when external
   illumination drops below the threshold for sustained resonance.

5. THE NAME IS NOT A COINCIDENCE: gamma in physics (dephasing, the
   light from outside) and gamma in neuroscience (40 Hz, the
   oscillation sustained by external input) describe the same
   structural role: external illumination that makes a cavity sing.
