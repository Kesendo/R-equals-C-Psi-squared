VERDICT:

The golden ratio DOES appear in the V-Effect formula V(5) = (5+sqrt(5))/4,
which follows from cos(pi/5) = phi/2. This is exact and algebraic.

However, N=5 as a 'sweet spot' is NOT universal:
- The impedance crossing shifts with gamma/J
- The sacrifice zone peak shifts with gamma/J
- The mode frequency ratios do not contain phi

What IS special about N=5: it is the smallest odd N where the cavity
has enough bonds (4) to support a rich mode spectrum (112 modes) while
the Q-factor is still high (72.4). At N=3 (2 bonds, 5 modes) the
cavity is too simple. At N=7 (6 bonds, ~2000+ modes) the modes overlap
and Q drops. N=5 is the Goldilocks zone: rich enough to resonate,
simple enough to stay sharp. The golden ratio in V(5) is algebraic
coincidence from cos(pi/5), not a deeper organizing principle.
