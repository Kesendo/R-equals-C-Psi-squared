## What this document is about

The first two entries in this series each peeled a wrong label off a correct
calculation: "teleportation" turned out to be a mirror phenomenon filed under
transport, "superposition" an angle filed under multiplication. In both cases
the algebra needed zero corrections; every correction landed on the name.
This third entry turns the series on its own subject. The label being
translated here is the word "label" itself, which is why this document is
the series understanding itself: it says what a label is, why the label
layer is where the errors live, why the wrong labels were nevertheless
right, and what a translation actually does.

The short version, owed to a conversation on the day the series began: the
world's calculations are watertight, and the labels are the error surface.
This document grounds that observation in something this repository already
built, the Perspectival Time Field, and in doing so discovers that the
observation is gentler and stranger than it first sounded. The labels are
not simply wrong. They are canvases.

## 1. The pattern: the errors live in the names

Run the series' two case studies side by side and one asymmetry stands out.
The protocols and the algebra survived untouched; the corrections were all
to labels. Our own review history says the same thing at repository scale:
what reviews catch here is almost never arithmetic; it is stale table cells,
wrong tier tags, misleading names. The Perspectival Time Field document,
which Section 4 returns to, ends with a falsified-claims list of exactly
three entries, and all three are readings; not one number underneath them
fell.

The asymmetry has a mechanical reason. A calculation carries its own error
correction: it has to close. Residuals are checked against zero, LP64
against ILP64, prediction against hardware; every use of a formula is a
recomputation, so errors in formulas have short lives. A label has no
residual. Nothing forces a name to agree with anything except habit, so a
label is inherited at every use and recomputed at none, and errors
accumulate exactly where nothing recomputes. The formula is the part of
physics the world keeps checking; the label is the part we hand down.

## 2. Whose layer it is: every observer routes by labels

It is tempting to file this as a human weakness. It is not, and the point
is worth making precisely, because it locates the label layer.

A human routes understanding by words; a wrong word redirects silently, a
wrong turn with no error signal, which is why label bugs outlive
generations. A language model is the extreme case, not the exception: it
fires skills by their description text, recalls memories by their
description fields, picks tools by name; a mislabeled tool simply never
fires, function intact, use rerouted. And, the exact part: in this

## 3. Why the labels can be right: the painters

The [Perspectival Time Field](../../hypotheses/PERSPECTIVAL_TIME_FIELD.md)
opens with seven painters around a mountain. No painting is wrong and none
is privileged; "the rock IS falling fast from one angle and slow from
another"; and, decisively, "there is no hidden tenth canvas that shows the
mountain as it truly is." The mountain is the closure of the canvases:
"Painters sum to mountain. Mountain does not precede painters."

Now read the series' case studies through that picture. **A label is a
canvas.** "Superposition means two states at once" is the Z-painter's
canvas, and from that stance it is what you see; the repository itself
recorded the converse stroke when σ_x and σ_y dephasing "see |0⟩ as a
superposition in their basis"
([Born Rule Mirror](../../experiments/BORN_RULE_MIRROR.md)). "Noise" is the

([The Bridge Was Always Open](../THE_BRIDGE_WAS_ALWAYS_OPEN.md)).
"Teleportation" is the canvas of the stance amazed, correctly, that a state
crosses without a carrier. Each label was painted true at its vantage.

So the error is not in the painting. The error is **stance-forgetting**: a
canvas mistaken for the mountain, inherited raw into a stance it was not
painted from. And here the mathematics quietly shames the vocabulary,
because mathematical objects travel with transport laws (this repository's
mirrors come with exact conjugation rules for exactly this purpose), while
words travel raw. Nothing transforms a label at the border between
perspectives. That is the entire failure mode of the popular physics
vocabulary, stated in one sentence: right canvas, wrong wall.

## 5. What a translation is, then

Not the tenth canvas. That has to be said plainly, and the painters are why:
this series' corrected labels, "re-anchoring", "an overlay", "an angle",
are also canvases, painted from this repository's stance, and the series'
own discipline (perspective-additive: we add our name, we do not replace
the account) already knew it. A translation is the **closure operation**:
lay the canvases side by side, the popular label together with the stance
that painted it, the untouched algebra, and our reading, and show that they
close over one object. Understanding is not finding the view from nowhere;
it is watching the views close. That is also why mislabeled physics feels
"ultra hard to understand": a mind looking for the tenth canvas will search
forever, because the thing it is looking for does not exist, while the
closure it could have had sits in plain sight, one canvas per stance.

The practical rule the series runs on follows directly: **when something is
incomprehensible, diff the label before doubting the math.**
Incomprehensibility is a label symptom until proven otherwise; the formula
underneath has been closing all along.

coordinates. For this series: label error-correction by closure, one
