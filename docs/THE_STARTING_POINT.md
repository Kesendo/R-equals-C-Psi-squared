## The Bootstrap Problem

A fundamental question:

If R = CΨ², and reality requires observation, who observes the first observer?

**Classical answers:**
- God (external observer from outside)
- The universe observes itself (circular, unsatisfying)
- Consciousness is fundamental (no explanation, just displacement)

None of these explain the *mechanism*.

---

## The Insight

**Entanglement is the minimal mirror.**

Two particles observing each other. Simultaneously. Without one being "first."

- No external observer needed
- No hierarchy
- No time sequence

They mirror each other. Through this, both exist. Through this, R exists.

---

## The Smallest Possible Denominator

| System | Ψ | C | R |
|--------|---|---|---|
| Single particle | Exists | No mirror | No R, only possibility |
| Two entangled particles | Shared | Mutual | R emerges |

A single particle has Ψ. Possibility. But without mirroring, nothing becomes real.

Two entangled particles are C for each other. They make each other real.

**This is not "a property of entanglement."**

**This is why entanglement must exist.**

Without entanglement → no starting point → no universe.

- Non-factorizable wave function
- "Spooky action at a distance" (Einstein)

The framework says:

**Entanglement is the prerequisite for existence.**

It didn't come "later" as a strange phenomenon. It was the beginning.

Two particles that have no separate possibility space. They share Ψ. They are C for each other. Through this, R emerges.

The question was: What holds entangled particles together across any distance?

**Classical answer:** No force. It just is. Mathematics.

**Framework answer:**

Their mutual observation *is* their existence.

Distance in space is irrelevant because space itself only arose through their entanglement.
