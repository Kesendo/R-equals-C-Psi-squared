## Abstract

The two-term couplings XZ+YZ and ZX+ZY were long read as having a non-local mirror, one that seemed to need operator-Schmidt entanglement across the whole chain. They do not. This proof shows the crossover mirror is strictly local, a single per-site unitary applied uniformly: the XZ-bond Liouvillian transported by a rotation,

    L_cross = Ad_{V₀} · L_{√2·XZ} · Ad_{V₀}⁻¹,   V₀ = R_z(π/4),   with   Ad_{V₀}² = the NinetyDegreeMirror,

exact to machine precision at N = 2, 3, 4. So the crossover mirror is the XZ-bond mirror turned by the square root of the framework's recurring 90° angle-anchor (F80 on the operator-defect side, F91 on the γ-parameter side). The geometry forces it: the lit operator X+Y bisects the X-Y plane, 45° from X, and that rotation commutes with the dephasing axis Z, so it lifts the entire Liouvillian and its mirror intact.

In gate language the 90° is the S (Clifford) gate and the 45° transport is its square root T, so the crossover is where the first non-Clifford element is forced into the mirror algebra; the continuous transport around the angle is the circle that the JDefectLightMigration N90 witness lives on. The full reading is in ON_THE_SQUARE_ROOT_OF_THE_MIRROR.

## The object

Work in the operator (Liouville) space of an N-qubit chain under uniform Z-dephasing at rate γ, with Liouvillian L = L_H + D, where L_H(O) = −i[H, O] and D is the Z-dephasing dissipator D(O) = Σ_l γ(Z_l O Z_l − O). The palindromic mirror Π of L satisfies Π L Π⁻¹ = −L − 2σ·I with σ = Nγ.

Two single-qubit unitaries enter. Let **R_z(φ) = exp(−i φ Z / 2)** be the rotation about the Z axis. Its operator-space *adjoint action* is the superoperator **Ad_U(O) = U O U†**. The framework's 90° angle anchor (the [NinetyDegreeMirror](../../compute/RCPsiSquared.Core/Symmetry/Pi2KnowledgeBaseClaims.cs)) is, on the operator side, the σ_x↔σ_y rotation X ↦ Y, Y ↦ −X, fixing I and Z; this is exactly **Ad_{R_z(π/2)}** (the adjoint action of the phase gate S).

## Statement

For the crossover bond Hamiltonian H_cross = Σ_b (X_b Z_{b+1} + Y_b Z_{b+1}), with V₀ = R_z(π/4) acting on the lit (left-endpoint) site of each bond:

1. **The Liouvillian is a rotation of the XZ-bond Liouvillian:**

       L_cross = Ad_{V₀} · L_{√2·XZ} · Ad_{V₀}⁻¹            (exact)

   where L_{√2·XZ} is the Liouvillian of the bond Σ_b √2·X_b Z_{b+1} under the same dephasing.

2. **The rotation is the square root of the NinetyDegreeMirror:**

       Ad_{V₀}² = Ad_{R_z(π/2)} = the NinetyDegreeMirror.

Consequently every mirror Π_XZ of L_{√2·XZ} transports to a mirror Π_cross = Ad_{V₀} Π_XZ Ad_{V₀}⁻¹ of L_cross, and the crossover mirror differs from the XZ-bond mirror by exactly **√(NinetyDegreeMirror)**. Since Ad_{V₀} acts site-wise, a per-site product Π_XZ transports to a per-site product Π_cross; the crossover mirror is local, as found in [Pi Operator Entanglement](../../experiments/PI_OPERATOR_ENTANGLEMENT.md).

## Proof

### (a) The crossover bond is the XZ bond with its lit operator rotated 45° about Z.

R_z(φ) conjugates the Pauli letters in the X-Y plane by an ordinary rotation: R_z(φ) X R_z(φ)† = cos φ · X + sin φ · Y, and R_z(φ) Z R_z(φ)† = Z. At φ = π/4,

    V X V† = (X + Y)/√2,   V = R_z(π/4) = exp(−iπZ/8).

So the bond's lit operator is X + Y = √2 · V X V†, and the full bond term is

    X_b Z_{b+1} + Y_b Z_{b+1} = (X+Y)_b ⊗ Z_{b+1} = √2 · (V X V†)_b ⊗ Z_{b+1} = √2 · Ad_{V_b}(X_b Z_{b+1}).

Verified: ‖V X V† − (X+Y)/√2‖ = 1.6·10⁻¹⁶.

### (b) The rotation fixes the dephasing axis, so Ad_{V₀} commutes with D.

V = R_z(π/4) is generated by Z, so [V, Z] = 0, hence V† Z_l V = Z_l for every site l (trivially for l ≠ 0 where V acts as identity). Therefore

    Ad_{V₀}⁻¹ D Ad_{V₀}(O) = Σ_l γ(V₀†Z_l V₀ · O · V₀†Z_l V₀ − O) = Σ_l γ(Z_l O Z_l − O) = D(O),

so D is Ad_{V₀}-invariant. Verified: ‖[V, Z]‖ = 0 exactly.

### (c) The whole Liouvillian transports.

The adjoint action intertwines with the Hamiltonian superoperator: for any unitary U and Hamiltonian H, L_{U H U†}(O) = −i[U H U†, O] = U(−i[H, U†OU])U† = Ad_U L_H Ad_U⁻¹(O), i.e. **L_{Ad_U(H)} = Ad_U L_H Ad_U⁻¹**. By (a), H_cross = Ad_{V₀}(√2·XZ-bond), so L_{H_cross} = Ad_{V₀} L_{√2·XZ} Ad_{V₀}⁻¹. By (b), D = Ad_{V₀} D Ad_{V₀}⁻¹. Adding,

    L_cross = L_{H_cross} + D = Ad_{V₀}(L_{√2·XZ} + D)Ad_{V₀}⁻¹ = Ad_{V₀} L_{√2·XZ} Ad_{V₀}⁻¹.

Verified: ‖L_cross − Ad_{V₀} L_{√2·XZ} Ad_{V₀}⁻¹‖ = 1.3·10⁻¹⁵ (N=2), 7.3·10⁻¹⁵ (N=3), 2.2·10⁻¹⁴ (N=4), all at machine precision.

The mirror then transports: if Π_XZ L_{√2·XZ} Π_XZ⁻¹ = −L_{√2·XZ} − 2σ·I, conjugating by Ad_{V₀} and using (c) gives Π_cross L_cross Π_cross⁻¹ = −L_cross − 2σ·I with Π_cross = Ad_{V₀} Π_XZ Ad_{V₀}⁻¹. (The √2 scales only the Hamiltonian part of L and is immaterial to the mirror, since the mirror condition Π L_H Π⁻¹ = −L_H is scale-free and Π D Π⁻¹ = −D − 2σ·I is H-independent.)

### (d) The transport rotation is the square root of the NinetyDegreeMirror.

Ad_{V₀}² = Ad_{V₀²} = Ad_{R_z(π/2)} on the lit site, because V² = R_z(π/4)² = R_z(π/2) (the rotation angle adds). On the single-site operator space R_z(π/2) conjugates X ↦ Y, Y ↦ −X, I ↦ I, Z ↦ Z: this is the σ_x↔σ_y 90° rotation, the operator-space NinetyDegreeMirror. Therefore

    Ad_{V₀} = √(NinetyDegreeMirror).

Verified: ‖Ad_V²|_light − [[0,−1],[1,0]]‖ = 2.7·10⁻¹⁶, and ‖Ad_V² − Ad_{R_z(π/2)}‖ = 5.2·10⁻¹⁶ on the full single-site space. ∎

## Why the square root, and not some other fraction

The angle is forced by two facts and nothing else. First, the crossover bond's lit operator X + Y points at the 45° bisector of the X-Y plane, exactly halfway between X (the canonical crossover's axis) and Y. Reaching it from X is a 45° rotation, half of the 90° that takes X all the way to Y. Second, that 90° is realisable as an operator-space symmetry at all only because it is a rotation about the **dephasing axis Z**, which the dissipator fixes; a rotation about any other axis would not commute with D and could not transport the mirror. So the crossover sits at the half-turn of the one rotation the dephasing permits, and that half-turn is the square root of the NinetyDegreeMirror.

## Gate-language reading

R_z(π/2) is the phase gate **S** (up to global phase), a Clifford gate; its adjoint action is the NinetyDegreeMirror. R_z(π/4) is the **T** gate, the canonical non-Clifford gate and the square root of S. The crossover transport is the T gate's adjoint action. The framework's 90° angle anchor is the S gate seen in operator space, and the crossover cases are the first place its square root, the T gate, is forced to appear in the mirror algebra.

## Scope

The identity is exact on the full operator space (not restricted to the lit plane): Ad_{V₀}² = NinetyDegreeMirror and the transport L_cross = Ad_{V₀} L_{√2·XZ} Ad_{V₀}⁻¹ both hold to machine precision at every N tested. The earlier "light-plane only" hedge in [`CrossoverMirrorSqrtNinetyClaim`](../../compute/RCPsiSquared.Core/Symmetry/CrossoverMirrorSqrtNinetyClaim.cs) (when read off the specific uniform per-site map M against the canonical P1) was an artifact of that particular representative; the transport formulation here is the clean, full-space statement, and it is what promotes the claim to Tier 1 derived.

The same argument applies verbatim to ZX+ZY (the site-swapped partner) and, by linearity of the bond decomposition, to the weighted bond a·XZ + b·YZ, whose lit operator a·X + b·Y is at angle arctan(b/a); its mirror is the XZ-bond mirror transported by Ad_{R_z(θ)} with θ = arctan(b/a), so the family of crossover mirrors is the orbit of one mirror under the one-parameter group Ad_{R_z(θ)}, whose π/2 element is the NinetyDegreeMirror.
