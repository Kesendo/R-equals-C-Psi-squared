## §1 Statement and definitions

Fix n ≥ 5. The comb is the mode set {1, …, n−1} with energies cos(kπ/n). A triple τ = {k₁ < k₂ < k₃} has level S(τ) = Σᵢ cos(kᵢπ/n). Call τ **clean** if no internal pair is balanced (kᵢ + kⱼ ≠ n for all pairs); the element n/2 alone (even n) is permitted.

**Theorem (F129, the level-collision law).** Let τ ≠ σ be clean triples with S(τ) = S(σ). Then **3 | n or 10 | n**, unconditionally (the one corner family the 2026-07-14 case analysis left open, a minimal weight-12 sum whose smallest ratio-order primes are {2,5,7}, is EMPTY by the Poonen-Rubinstein weight ≤ 12 classification: closed 2026-07-15, §4; independently certified computationally through n = 210). Conversely, such pairs exist at every n with 3|n, n ≥ 9, and at every n with 10|n, n ≥ 20 (exhibited exactly for every such n ≤ 210 by gate G3; beyond that, by the two explicit families of §4). Equivalently: away from {3|n} ∪ {10|n} the level map is injective on clean triples.

**Sub-law (the overlap refinement).** If τ and σ share a mode (|τ∩σ| = 1, the 4-term case), then already 3 | n. The 10|n door is walked only by fully disjoint pairs.

Mechanisms: at 3|n the collisions are built from rotated 3-cycles of 2n-th roots of unity (with the Conway-Jones 15|n sporadics as an absorbed sub-family); at 10|n they come through the pentagon family, a conjugate pair of rotated 5-cycles plus the zero mode n/2 (at 70|n the classification in §4 permits exactly one further assembly, an (R₇:R₅) piece plus the zero mode; the law is unaffected). In the seed arc's three-cosine world the pentagon only ever appeared chained to the 3 (the 15|n sporadic (R₅:R₃), `Seed.cs`); at six cosines it is unchained.

## §2 The reduction: equal sums are vanishing sums

Cancel the shared modes: A = τ∖σ, B = σ∖τ, |A| = |B| = d ∈ {1, 2, 3} (d = 1 is impossible: it would force cos(kπ/n) = cos(lπ/n) with k ≠ l in (0, n)). Because cos((n−l)π/n) = −cos(lπ/n),

  S(τ) = S(σ) ⟺ Σ_{e ∈ E} cos(eπ/n) = 0, E = A ⊎ {n−l : l ∈ B},

a multiset of 2d elements of (0, n) (an element can appear twice, when a cross pair k ∈ A, l ∈ B has k + l = n; that is not excluded and not trivial). Cleanliness translates exactly: an antipodal pair {e, n−e} of *distinct* elements inside E would be an internal balanced pair of τ (both in A) or of σ (both reflected), both excluded; the one-of-each case cannot arise at all, since e ∈ A and n−e reflected would force e ∈ A ∩ B = ∅. The one self-antipodal element n/2 can appear at most once (gate G1 verifies the two-way reduction exactly on random pairs).

In root language, with ζ = e^{iπ/n} of order m = 2n:

  W = Σ_{e ∈ E} (ζ^e + ζ^{−e}) = 0,

a vanishing sum of 4d ∈ {8, 12} roots of unity with nonnegative coefficients whose support is **conjugation-symmetric** (closed under x ↦ −x), contains **no real root** (exponents avoid 0 and n), and admits **no 2-cycle** {ζ^a, ζ^{a+n}} except the zero-mode pair {ζ^{n/2}, ζ^{−n/2}} = {i, −i}, at most once (a 2-cycle between distinct elements is exactly an antipodal pair).

## §3 The decomposition frame

The **weight** of a sum of roots of unity is its number of terms counted with multiplicity; μ_p denotes the group of p-th roots of unity, so a rotated p-cycle R_p is a coset η·μ_p summing to zero. The **order** of a sum is the rotation-invariant one: the order of the group generated by the RATIOS of its terms (for a rotated p-cycle that is exactly p, whatever η is); since all our terms lie in μ_{2n}, every piece's order divides 2n. Every vanishing sum with nonnegative coefficients splits into **minimal** vanishing sums (ones with no proper vanishing sub-multiset; induction on weight). Three imported facts about minimal sums, all from Lam-Leung, *On vanishing sums of roots of unity*, J. Algebra 224 (2000), stated for this ratio order:

- **(LL1)** the weight of any vanishing sum of m-th roots lies in ℕp₁ + … + ℕp_k over the primes pᵢ | m;
- **(LL2)** a minimal vanishing sum of m-th roots with m = p^a q^b (at most **two** prime divisors) is a rotated p-cycle R_p or q-cycle R_q, no exotics (so a minimal piece whose ratio order has ≤ 2 prime divisors is a single rotated cycle, and its ratio order is then one prime);
- **(LL3)** if the ratio order of a minimal sum has **at least three** prime divisors p₁ < p₂ < p₃ < …, its weight is at least (p₁−1)(p₂−1) + p₃ − 1 (this bound is saturated by the Conway-Jones weight-6 sporadic at {2,3,5}).

(Source pins, corrected 2026-07-15 after a referee round caught the original pointers wrong; Lam-Leung numbering per the arXiv preprint math/9511209, against which we verified the statements: (LL1) is Lam-Leung's Main Theorem 5.2; (LL2) is Lam-Leung's Corollary 3.4 (their m = pᵃqᵇ); the relevant sub-cases also appear as Lemma 3.1 and, as a specialization, Theorem 3.2 of the classification paper arXiv:2008.11268 (Christie-Dykema-Klep, not a survey); (LL3) is Lam-Leung's Lower Bound Theorem 4.8, stated there for ASYMMETRIC minimal elements as ε(x) ≥ p₁(p₂−1) + p₃ − p₂, algebraically equal to our form, and it applies to every minimal piece whose ratio order has ≥ 3 primes because the symmetric minimal elements are exactly the rotated single prime cycles, whose ratio order has one prime; that paper does not restate an LL3 analogue, and the earlier pointer "Theorems 2.3/2.4 of the survey" pointed at a proposition and a definition that say neither thing. At weight ≤ 12 every use of (LL3) below is additionally covered by the complete classification imported in §4, so the corner closure does not lean on (LL3) alone.)

Two elementary structural facts about pieces of our W:

- **(Pairing)** For a p-cycle piece R ⊆ W (a coset of μ_p): conj(R) ⊆ W too (conjugation permutes W's multiset), and distinct μ_p-cosets are disjoint. If conj(R) = R, the exponent involution e ↦ −e (complex conjugation) on an odd number of elements has a fixed point, a real root, which W does not contain. So for odd p every p-cycle comes in disjoint conjugate **pairs**, costing weight 2p.
- **(Complement)** If a piece has weight w, the rest of W is a vanishing sum of weight 4d − w; weight 1 is impossible, so w ≠ 4d − 1.

## §4 The case analysis

Let P be any minimal piece of W, with (ratio) order d_P | 2n and prime set π(P) = {p : p | d_P}.

1. **3 ∈ π(P).** Then 3 | 2n, and since 3 is odd, **3 | n**: the law holds. (This absorbs every 3-touching piece at once: all R₃'s, the Conway-Jones weight-6 sporadic, whose ratio order is 30 with prime set {2,3,5} and which needs 15|n to fit inside μ_{2n}, and every other 15|n and 30|n exotic. No inventory needed.)
2. **|π(P)| ≤ 2, 3 ∉ π(P).** By (LL2) (whose two-prime hypothesis covers every d_P = p^a q^b, and whose one-prime case is classical), P is a rotated single cycle R_p, and its ratio order is then exactly p; so the ≤2-prime cases collapse to cycles: **R₂** (a 2-cycle: only the zero mode, at most once, weight 2, existing only for even n); **R₅** (then 5 | 2n, so **5 | n**; by (Pairing), R₅'s come in disjoint conjugate pairs of weight 10); **R₇ or R₁₁** ((Pairing) demands weight 14 or 22 > 12: dead); **R_p, p ≥ 13** (weight p > 12: dead).
3. **|π(P)| ≥ 3, 3 ∉ π(P).** By (LL3), the weight is at least the bound from the three SMALLEST primes of π(P): {2,5,7} → ≥ (1)(4)+6 = 10; {2,5,11} → ≥ 14; {2,7,11} → ≥ 16; {5,7,11} → ≥ 34. So the three smallest primes must be exactly {2, 5, 7} (further primes ≥ 11 in π(P) are NOT excluded by the bound), the weight is 10 or 12 (11 is excluded by (Complement)), and 5, 7 ∈ π(P) force **5 | n and 7 | n**.

Now assemble, remembering the total weight is 8 or 12 and the zero mode exists only for **even n**:

- **Weight 8 (the shared-mode case).** No R₅-pair fits (10 > 8), no case-3 piece fits (≥ 10 > 8), R₂ beyond the zero mode is excluded, and 8 cannot be composed from the remaining inventory without a 3-piece. Hence some piece has 3 ∈ π(P): **the sub-law, 4-term collisions force 3|n**.
- **Weight 12, no 3-piece.** The inventory is: R₅-conjugate-pairs (10), the zero mode (2), case-3 pieces (10 or 12). Partitions of 12: **10 + 2** (an R₅-pair or a weight-10 case-3 piece, plus the zero mode) or **12** (a single case-3 piece). In the 10+2 partitions the zero mode forces n even and the R₅/case-3 piece forces 5|n: **10 | n**. In the single-piece-12 partition, 5|n and 7|n hold; if n is even this is again 10|n. The one residual corner is:

**The named corner.** A single **minimal** weight-12 vanishing sum, conjugation-symmetric, real-root-free, whose ratio order has three smallest primes {2, 5, 7} (further prime factors ≥ 11 are possible; Lam-Leung's bound does not exclude them), at **odd** n with 35 | n and 3 ∤ n. Every instance of the corner, with or without extra primes, forces 35 | n and n odd, so the whole family lives on one arithmetic progression. On the night of 2026-07-14 we did not close this corner analytically; the census (§6) certified it from below at the two in-range candidates n = 35 and 175 (both non-firing, levels pairwise distinct; n = 105 fires via 3|n and is not a corner case; the next candidate is n = 245 > 210, extra-prime variants from n ≥ 385). We named it rather than silently citing it, noting that the Conway-Jones/Poonen-Rubinstein classifications of small vanishing sums could settle it.

**Closed 2026-07-15: the corner family is empty.** They do settle it. The classification of ALL minimal vanishing sums of weight ≤ 12, up to rotation (Poonen-Rubinstein, *The number of intersection points made by the diagonals of a regular polygon*, SIAM J. Discrete Math. 11 (1998), extending Conway-Jones from weight ≤ 9; we verified it against Theorem 3.3 + Table 1 of the classification paper arXiv:2008.11268 cited for (LL2) above, which reproduces the weight ≤ 12 list and extends it by hand to weight ≤ 16; that theorem classifies ALL minimal sums with multiplicities allowed and proves each has HEIGHT 1, all coefficients +1, closing the one subtlety §2 flagged: W may carry one element with coefficient 2, and a minimal weight-12 piece with a repeated term would still have to be one of the listed types, none of which repeats a term), contains exactly five types of weight 12: (R₁₁:R₃) of relative order 66 = 2·3·11, three types of order 210 = 2·3·5·7, and (R₇:5R₃) of order 42 = 2·3·7. **Every weight-12 type has 3 dividing its order**, and a minimal sum's relative (ratio) order is squarefree and is what its π(P) reads off; the corner demands 3 ∉ π(P). So no corner instance exists, with or without extra primes ≥ 11 (an extra-prime instance would still be a weight-12 minimal sum and hence in the complete list). The single-piece-12 partition of §4 is therefore impossible without a 3, and the law is **unconditional**. Two sanity anchors from the same table: the only type of any weight ≤ 12 whose order both 5 and 7 divide while 3 does not is (R₇:R₅), weight 10, order 70 = 2·5·7, saturating the (LL3) bound for {2,5,7} (the bare R₇ shares the 7-without-3 but not the 5); it cannot be all of W (weight 12), and in the 10+2 partition its zero-mode partner forces n even, landing inside 10 | n, the second mechanism already flagged in §1. And an order divisible by 5 and 7 but by neither 2 nor 3 never occurs at any weight ≤ 16 (the 5·7-carrying orders through weight 16 are 70, 210, and one order-105 type at weight 16, which carries the 3). The n ≤ 210 census (§6), in particular the exact injectivity at n = 35 and 175, now stands as the independent from-below cross-check of this import rather than as the load-bearing certificate. Every branch of the case analysis is complete.

**Converses.** At 3|n, n ≥ 9: two disjoint rotated-R₃ folds give clean equal-level pairs (gate G3 exhibits one exact collision at every firing n; gate G5a anchors n = 15, where (8,12,14) ~ (9,11,13) at level ≈ −1.892 decomposes into four rotated 3-cycles, exactly). At 10|n, n ≥ 20: the pentagon family (gate G5b anchors n = 20, where (1,7,9) ~ (3,5,10) decomposes into an R₅ conjugate pair plus the zero mode, exactly). ∎
