## Setup

**The model.** The single-excitation sector of an N-qubit XX chain (or the XX+YY part of any Heisenberg/XXZ chain) under local Z-dephasing reduces to a tight-binding lattice on N sites: a vector |ПҲвҹ© = ОЈ_в„“ c_в„“ |1_в„“вҹ© evolves under

    dПҒ/dt = вҲ’i [H, ПҒ] + L_D[ПҒ],

where H_{в„“m} = t_{(в„“,m)} for nearest-neighbour pairs and zero otherwise, and L_D[ПҒ]_{в„“m} = вҲ’Оі_eff(в„“, m) ПҒ_{в„“m} acts diagonally per matrix element with Оі_eff(в„“, в„“) = 0 and Оі_eff(в„“, m) > 0 for в„“ вү  m.

For uniform Z-dephasing Оі_в„“ = Оі on all sites, the single-excitation L_D reduces to Оі_eff(в„“, m) = 4Оі for в„“ вү  m, 0 for в„“ = m. For non-uniform Оі_в„“ the effective rate Оі_eff(в„“, m) = 2(Оі_в„“ + Оі_m) for в„“ вү  m. Either profile is K-invariant under the argument below.

**Class of H this proof covers.** L1 below requires only the bipartite NN-hopping structure; it applies to:
- The XX or XX+YY chain in the single-excitation sector. F65's ПҲ_k are eigenvectors of this H.
- Any tight-binding hopping model on a bipartite graph with arbitrary t_в„“ profile.

It does **not** cover:
- Hamiltonians with on-site potential V_в„“ (breaks L1 directly).
- Hamiltonians with NNN hopping (NNN bonds connect same-sublattice sites; (вҲ’1)^{в„“+m} = +1 fails to negate H_{в„“m}).
- Heisenberg/XXZ О” вү  0 on **open** chains: in the single-excitation sector the ZZ-term contributes V_eff(в„“) = (#bonds) вҲ’ 2В·deg(в„“). Open chains have deg(0) = deg(N-1) = 1 < deg(interior) = 2, generating an effective on-site potential at the boundary that breaks L1. On periodic chains with uniform deg, V_eff is a constant shift and L1 is restored.

**Two symmetries are needed for the observable identity.** The chiral symmetry K alone gives spectrum inversion (L1, L2). The **observable identity** in L4 below additionally requires anti-unitary time-reversal T = complex conjugation with TВІ = +I. Both are present whenever H is real and the system is in AZ class **BDI** (TRSВІ = +I, PHSВІ = +I, chiral S = TВ·K). On complex hopping (Peierls phase), TRS breaks; the system drops to AZ class **AIII** (chiral only) and partner observables differ in phase.

## Proof

### Lemma 1 (KHK = вҲ’H for bipartite NN-hopping)

In the site basis K is diagonal with K_{в„“в„“} = (вҲ’1)^в„“. Then

    (KHK)_{в„“m} = (вҲ’1)^в„“ H_{в„“m} (вҲ’1)^m = (вҲ’1)^{в„“+m} H_{в„“m}.

H_{в„“m} вү  0 only for |в„“ вҲ’ m| = 1, where (вҲ’1)^{в„“+m} = (вҲ’1)^{2 min(в„“,m) + 1} = вҲ’1. Hence

    KHK = вҲ’H.   вҲҺ

This holds for any t_в„“ profile. The bipartite requirement is precisely that every edge connects two sublattices of opposite K-parity. NNN hopping connects same-sublattice sites and is therefore not negated.

### Lemma 2 (Spectrum inversion)

Apply Lemma 1 to an eigenvector ПҲ_k of H with eigenvalue E_k:

    H (KПҲ_k) = (KHK)(K В· K) ПҲ_k = вҲ’H В· ПҲ_k = вҲ’E_k (KПҲ_k)   (using KВІ = I in the second factor).

So KПҲ_k is an eigenvector with eigenvalue вҲ’E_k. F65's spectrum Оұ_k/ОівӮҖ = (4/(N+1)) sinВІ(kПҖ/(N+1)) for the uniform open chain has its "internal mirror" Оұ_k = Оұ_{N+1-k} as an arithmetic identity; the K theorem identifies it as a structural symmetry under H-anticommutation. By spectrum-bijection KПҲ_k вҲқ ПҲ_{N+1-k}, with phase fixed up to Вұ1 by orthonormality.   вҲҺ

### Lemma 3 (Concrete bonding-mode swap, uniform J)

For ПҲ_k(в„“) = вҲҡ(2/(N+1)) sin(ПҖk(в„“+1)/(N+1)),

    (KПҲ_k)(в„“) = (вҲ’1)^в„“ вҲҡ(2/(N+1)) sin(ПҖk(в„“+1)/(N+1)).

Using sin(ПҖ(в„“+1) вҲ’ x) = (вҲ’1)^в„“ sin(x) (because sin(ПҖ(в„“+1)) = 0 and cos(ПҖ(в„“+1)) = (вҲ’1)^{в„“+1}):

    sin(ПҖ(N+1-k)(в„“+1)/(N+1)) = sin(ПҖ(в„“+1) вҲ’ ПҖk(в„“+1)/(N+1)) = (вҲ’1)^в„“ sin(ПҖk(в„“+1)/(N+1)).

Hence (KПҲ_k)(в„“) = ПҲ_{N+1-k}(в„“) without extra phase factor.   вҲҺ

### Lemma 4 (Mirror-pair |В·|ВІ-observable identity, real H)

Define ПҒМғ_k(t) := K ПҒ_k(t) K. By K-conjugating both sides of dПҒ_k/dt = L[ПҒ_k]:

    dПҒМғ_k/dt = K L[K ПҒМғ_k K] K
             = вҲ’i [KHK, ПҒМғ_k] + (K L_D[K ПҒМғ_k K] K)
             = +i [H, ПҒМғ_k] + L_D[ПҒМғ_k]   (using L1 and L_D being K-invariant per matrix element).

So ПҒМғ_k satisfies a Lindblad equation with the **sign-flipped** Hamiltonian вҲ’H and the **same** dissipator. Initial condition: ПҒМғ_k(0) = K|ПҲ_kвҹ©вҹЁПҲ_k|K = |KПҲ_kвҹ©вҹЁKПҲ_k| = |ПҲ_{N+1-k}вҹ©вҹЁПҲ_{N+1-k}| = ПҒ_{N+1-k}(0).

For **real** H (which holds for real-valued t_в„“; complex Peierls hopping is excluded here), complex-conjugating dПҒ_{N+1-k}/dt = L[ПҒ_{N+1-k}]:

    d ПҒ_{N+1-k}*/dt = (L[ПҒ_{N+1-k}])* = +i [H*, ПҒ_{N+1-k}*] + L_D*[ПҒ_{N+1-k}*] = +i [H, ПҒ_{N+1-k}*] + L_D[ПҒ_{N+1-k}*]

(the dissipator L_D acts with real rates Оі_eff(в„“, m) and is therefore real). So ПҒ_{N+1-k}(t)* satisfies the same Lindblad equation as ПҒМғ_k(t), namely +i[H, В·] + L_D, with identical initial condition (ПҒ_{N+1-k}(0) = (ПҒ_{N+1-k}(0))* because pure-state density matrices from real ПҲ are real). By uniqueness of the Lindblad flow:

    ПҒМғ_k(t) = ПҒ_{N+1-k}(t)*,   i.e.,   K ПҒ_k(t) K = ПҒ_{N+1-k}(t)*.

In components:

    (вҲ’1)^{в„“+m} ПҒ_k(t)_{в„“m} = ПҒ_{N+1-k}(t)_{в„“m}*.

**Diagonal (в„“ = m):**

    ПҒ_k(t)_{в„“в„“} = ПҒ_{N+1-k}(t)_{в„“в„“}*  =  ПҒ_{N+1-k}(t)_{в„“в„“}   (diagonal of Hermitian ПҒ is real).

Populations are identical pointwise.

**Off-diagonal (в„“ вү  m):**

    |ПҒ_k(t)_{в„“m}|ВІ = |ПҒ_{N+1-k}(t)_{в„“m}|ВІ.

Coherence moduli are identical; the relative phase differs by (вҲ’1)^{в„“+m} composed with complex conjugation.

Any observable expressible as a function of populations and coherence moduli is therefore K-partner invariant. Mirror-pair MI(a, N-1-a) constructs the 2-site reduced density matrix ПҒ_{ab} from p_a, p_b, and ПҒ_{ab}; its von Neumann entropy depends on eigenvalues that depend on |ПҒ_{ab}|ВІ (the coherence enters quadratically through det), so MI is invariant. The PTF-analog ОЈ_в„“ log ПҖ_в„“ depends only on populations and is invariant.   вҲҺ

**Phase-sensitive observables.** Re(ПҒ_{ab}(t)) for fixed a, b is **not** invariant: the sign (вҲ’1)^{a+b} composed with complex conjugation generally flips Re. The receiver-menu folding under K-partnership therefore applies to |В·|ВІ-observables only.

### Lemma 5 (K-Invariance of Z-dephasing dissipator)

L_D acts diagonally per matrix element: L_D[П„]_{в„“m} = вҲ’Оі_eff(в„“, m) П„_{в„“m}. Then

    (K L_D[K Пғ K] K)_{в„“m} = (вҲ’1)^{в„“+m} L_D[K Пғ K]_{в„“m} = (вҲ’1)^{в„“+m}(вҲ’Оі_eff)(K Пғ K)_{в„“m}
                          = (вҲ’1)^{в„“+m}(вҲ’Оі_eff)(вҲ’1)^{в„“+m} Пғ_{в„“m} = вҲ’Оі_eff Пғ_{в„“m} = L_D[Пғ]_{в„“m}.

So L_D = K L_D(K В· K) K, i.e., the dissipator commutes with K-conjugation. This holds for **any** profile {Оі_в„“}, including non-uniform Z-dephasing. Lemma 5 is what allows L4 to absorb arbitrary Оі_в„“-profiles without breaking partner identity.   вҲҺ

## AZ classification

K is a **chiral unitary symmetry**: K = KвҖ  = KвҒ»В№ with KВІ = I and KHK = вҲ’H. In Altland-Zirnbauer (ten-fold-way) terminology this is called *chiral* symmetry, often denoted S in the condensed-matter literature. K is **not** time-reversal (which is anti-unitary) and **not** particle-hole (also anti-unitary). The recurrent terminology error of calling KHK = вҲ’H "time reversal" is the reason the proof above explicitly tracks T = complex conjugation as a separate ingredient.

When H is real, the additional anti-unitary T (complex conjugation) gives TВІ = +I and [T, H] = 0. Combined with chiral S = K, the AZ classification is **class BDI**:

| Symmetry  | Operator        | Property              |
|-----------|-----------------|------------------------|
| TRS       | T (complex conj.) | TВІ = +I, [T, H] = 0  |
| PHS       | TВ·S             | (TВ·S)ВІ = +I            |
| Chiral    | S = K           | SВІ = +I, {S, H} = 0    |

For **complex** hopping (Peierls phase, t_в„“ вҲҲ в„Ӯ\в„қ), TRS breaks and the system drops to **class AIII** (chiral only). Spectrum inversion (L2) still holds, but the observable identity (L4) requires the complex conjugation step and fails: ПҒ_k(t) and ПҒ_{N+1-k}(t) have inverted spectrum but the populations may differ in time. This boundary between BDI and AIII is the structural reason the partner-identity table in HANDSHAKE_ALGEBRA needs *real* H, not just *bipartite* H.

## Scope and limitations

**Valid for:**

- Single-excitation sector of any bipartite NN-hopping Hamiltonian with real t_в„“ (uniform or non-uniform).
- Arbitrary site-dependent Z-dephasing Оі_в„“.
- Open boundary condition; periodic boundary with even N (preserves bipartite structure).
- Any |В·|ВІ-based observable on mirror-pairs (a, N-1-a): MI, classical correlation, log-purity, fidelity to symmetric reference states.

**Does NOT cover:**

- Multi-excitation sectors. Each fixed-particle-number sector still reduces to a tight-binding problem in the appropriate Fock space, but the on-site interaction terms generated by ZZ-coupling generally break L1 even on periodic chains.
- Phase-sensitive observables (Re(ПҒ_{ab}), Im(ПҒ_{ab}), holomorphic functions of ПҒ).
- Hamiltonians with on-site potential, NNN hopping, or non-bipartite topology.
- Complex hopping (no T-symmetry; only spectrum inversion survives).
- Heisenberg/XXZ О” вү  0 on **open** chains (deg-discontinuity at boundaries).

**Verified:**

- N = 9 single-excitation, four H-classes (uniform J, non-uniform J, +V_в„“ on-site, +NNN JвҖІ), two Оі regimes (0 and 0.1).

**Open:**

- Complex-hopping case (predicted spectrum-inversion-only by L2 absent T-symmetry).
- Extension to multi-excitation sectors.
- Heisenberg/XXZ О” вү  0 on open chain (predicted boundary breakdown).
- Larger N (limited only by the cost of the single-excitation reduction; trivial in the matrix-free regime).
