## Statement

Let H₁ ≅ ℂᴺ be the single-excitation Hilbert space of an N-site chain in the basis {|1_ℓ⟩}_{ℓ=0..N-1}, and define the **bipartite sublattice gauge**

    K = diag((−1)^ℓ),   K|1_ℓ⟩ = (−1)^ℓ |1_ℓ⟩.

Let H be any nearest-neighbor (NN) tight-binding Hamiltonian on H₁,

    H = Σ_{ℓ=0}^{N-2} t_ℓ (|1_ℓ⟩⟨1_{ℓ+1}| + |1_{ℓ+1}⟩⟨1_ℓ|),

with arbitrary real hopping amplitudes t_ℓ ∈ ℝ (uniform J or non-uniform). Let L_D be the single-excitation projection of any local Z-dephasing dissipator with site-dependent rates γ_ℓ ≥ 0. Then:

1. **Anticommutation.** KHK = −H.
2. **Spectrum inversion.** If H ψ_k = E_k ψ_k with non-degenerate spectrum, then K ψ_k ∝ ψ_{N+1-k} and E_{N+1-k} = −E_k.
3. **Bonding-mode swap.** For uniform J the F65 modes ψ_k(ℓ) = √(2/(N+1)) sin(πk(ℓ+1)/(N+1)) satisfy (Kψ_k)(ℓ) = ψ_{N+1-k}(ℓ) exactly (no extra phase).
4. **Mirror-pair |·|²-observable identity.** Under the full single-excitation Lindblad evolution generated by L = -i[H, ·] + L_D with **real** H, the trajectories ρ_k(t) = e^{Lt}|ψ_k⟩⟨ψ_k| and ρ_{N+1-k}(t) = e^{Lt}|ψ_{N+1-k}⟩⟨ψ_{N+1-k}| satisfy

       ρ_{N+1-k}(t) = (K ρ_k(t) K)*

   so site populations and coherence moduli agree pointwise for all t ≥ 0:

       (ρ_{N+1-k}(t))_{ℓℓ} = (ρ_k(t))_{ℓℓ},   |(ρ_{N+1-k}(t))_{ℓm}| = |(ρ_k(t))_{ℓm}|.

   Any observable that is a function of {(ρ)_{ℓℓ}} ∪ {|(ρ)_{ℓm}|²} is identical for the two trajectories. Mirror-pair MI(a, N-1-a)(t) and the PTF-analog Σ_ℓ log π_ℓ(t) are such observables.

The robustness pattern across H-extensions (entries 1-2 numerically verified, entries 3-6 predicted from L1-L2):

| H structure                                       | KHK = −H | Real H | Partner identity |
|---------------------------------------------------|----------|--------|------------------|
| Uniform J, NN hopping, any γ_ℓ-profile            | ✓        | ✓      | holds            |
| Non-uniform J_ℓ, NN hopping, any γ_ℓ-profile      | ✓        | ✓      | holds            |
| + on-site potential V_ℓ                           | ✗        | ✓      | breaks           |
| + NNN hopping (next-nearest neighbour)            | ✗        | ✓      | breaks           |
| Complex hopping (Peierls phase t_ℓ ∈ ℂ)           | ✓        | ✗      | spectrum inverts; observables not identical |
| Heisenberg/XXZ Δ ≠ 0 on **open** chain            | ✗        | ✓      | breaks at boundary (deg-discontinuity) |
| Heisenberg/XXZ Δ ≠ 0 on periodic chain, even N    | ✓        | ✓      | holds            |

## Setup

**The model.** The single-excitation sector of an N-qubit XX chain (or the XX+YY part of any Heisenberg/XXZ chain) under local Z-dephasing reduces to a tight-binding lattice on N sites: a vector |ψ⟩ = Σ_ℓ c_ℓ |1_ℓ⟩ evolves under

    dρ/dt = −i [H, ρ] + L_D[ρ],

where H_{ℓm} = t_{(ℓ,m)} for nearest-neighbour pairs and zero otherwise, and L_D[ρ]_{ℓm} = −γ_eff(ℓ, m) ρ_{ℓm} acts diagonally per matrix element with γ_eff(ℓ, ℓ) = 0 and γ_eff(ℓ, m) > 0 for ℓ ≠ m.

For uniform Z-dephasing γ_ℓ = γ on all sites, the single-excitation L_D reduces to γ_eff(ℓ, m) = 4γ for ℓ ≠ m, 0 for ℓ = m. For non-uniform γ_ℓ the effective rate γ_eff(ℓ, m) = 2(γ_ℓ + γ_m) for ℓ ≠ m. Either profile is K-invariant under the argument below.

**Class of H this proof covers.** L1 below requires only the bipartite NN-hopping structure; it applies to:
- The XX or XX+YY chain in the single-excitation sector. F65's ψ_k are eigenvectors of this H.
- Any tight-binding hopping model on a bipartite graph with arbitrary t_ℓ profile.

It does **not** cover:
- Hamiltonians with on-site potential V_ℓ (breaks L1 directly).
- Hamiltonians with NNN hopping (NNN bonds connect same-sublattice sites; (−1)^{ℓ+m} = +1 fails to negate H_{ℓm}).
- Heisenberg/XXZ Δ ≠ 0 on **open** chains: in the single-excitation sector the ZZ-term contributes V_eff(ℓ) = (#bonds) − 2·deg(ℓ). Open chains have deg(0) = deg(N-1) = 1 < deg(interior) = 2, generating an effective on-site potential at the boundary that breaks L1. On periodic chains with uniform deg, V_eff is a constant shift and L1 is restored.

**Two symmetries are needed for the observable identity.** The chiral symmetry K alone gives spectrum inversion (L1, L2). The **observable identity** in L4 below additionally requires anti-unitary time-reversal T = complex conjugation with T² = +I. Both are present whenever H is real and the system is in AZ class **BDI** (TRS² = +I, PHS² = +I, chiral S = T·K). On complex hopping (Peierls phase), TRS breaks; the system drops to AZ class **AIII** (chiral only) and partner observables differ in phase.

## Proof

### Lemma 1 (KHK = −H for bipartite NN-hopping)

In the site basis K is diagonal with K_{ℓℓ} = (−1)^ℓ. Then

    (KHK)_{ℓm} = (−1)^ℓ H_{ℓm} (−1)^m = (−1)^{ℓ+m} H_{ℓm}.

H_{ℓm} ≠ 0 only for |ℓ − m| = 1, where (−1)^{ℓ+m} = (−1)^{2 min(ℓ,m) + 1} = −1. Hence

    KHK = −H.   ∎

This holds for any t_ℓ profile. The bipartite requirement is precisely that every edge connects two sublattices of opposite K-parity. NNN hopping connects same-sublattice sites and is therefore not negated.

### Lemma 2 (Spectrum inversion)

Apply Lemma 1 to an eigenvector ψ_k of H with eigenvalue E_k:

    H (Kψ_k) = (KHK)(K · K) ψ_k = −H · ψ_k = −E_k (Kψ_k)   (using K² = I in the second factor).

So Kψ_k is an eigenvector with eigenvalue −E_k. F65's spectrum α_k/γ₀ = (4/(N+1)) sin²(kπ/(N+1)) for the uniform open chain has its "internal mirror" α_k = α_{N+1-k} as an arithmetic identity; the K theorem identifies it as a structural symmetry under H-anticommutation. By spectrum-bijection Kψ_k ∝ ψ_{N+1-k}, with phase fixed up to ±1 by orthonormality.   ∎

### Lemma 3 (Concrete bonding-mode swap, uniform J)

For ψ_k(ℓ) = √(2/(N+1)) sin(πk(ℓ+1)/(N+1)),

    (Kψ_k)(ℓ) = (−1)^ℓ √(2/(N+1)) sin(πk(ℓ+1)/(N+1)).

Using sin(π(ℓ+1) − x) = (−1)^ℓ sin(x) (because sin(π(ℓ+1)) = 0 and cos(π(ℓ+1)) = (−1)^{ℓ+1}):

    sin(π(N+1-k)(ℓ+1)/(N+1)) = sin(π(ℓ+1) − πk(ℓ+1)/(N+1)) = (−1)^ℓ sin(πk(ℓ+1)/(N+1)).

Hence (Kψ_k)(ℓ) = ψ_{N+1-k}(ℓ) without extra phase factor.   ∎

### Lemma 4 (Mirror-pair |·|²-observable identity, real H)

Define ρ̃_k(t) := K ρ_k(t) K. By K-conjugating both sides of dρ_k/dt = L[ρ_k]:

    dρ̃_k/dt = K L[K ρ̃_k K] K
             = −i [KHK, ρ̃_k] + (K L_D[K ρ̃_k K] K)
             = +i [H, ρ̃_k] + L_D[ρ̃_k]   (using L1 and L_D being K-invariant per matrix element).

So ρ̃_k satisfies a Lindblad equation with the **sign-flipped** Hamiltonian −H and the **same** dissipator. Initial condition: ρ̃_k(0) = K|ψ_k⟩⟨ψ_k|K = |Kψ_k⟩⟨Kψ_k| = |ψ_{N+1-k}⟩⟨ψ_{N+1-k}| = ρ_{N+1-k}(0).

For **real** H (which holds for real-valued t_ℓ; complex Peierls hopping is excluded here), complex-conjugating dρ_{N+1-k}/dt = L[ρ_{N+1-k}]:

    d ρ_{N+1-k}*/dt = (L[ρ_{N+1-k}])* = +i [H*, ρ_{N+1-k}*] + L_D*[ρ_{N+1-k}*] = +i [H, ρ_{N+1-k}*] + L_D[ρ_{N+1-k}*]

(the dissipator L_D acts with real rates γ_eff(ℓ, m) and is therefore real). So ρ_{N+1-k}(t)* satisfies the same Lindblad equation as ρ̃_k(t), namely +i[H, ·] + L_D, with identical initial condition (ρ_{N+1-k}(0) = (ρ_{N+1-k}(0))* because pure-state density matrices from real ψ are real). By uniqueness of the Lindblad flow:

    ρ̃_k(t) = ρ_{N+1-k}(t)*,   i.e.,   K ρ_k(t) K = ρ_{N+1-k}(t)*.

In components:

    (−1)^{ℓ+m} ρ_k(t)_{ℓm} = ρ_{N+1-k}(t)_{ℓm}*.

**Diagonal (ℓ = m):**

    ρ_k(t)_{ℓℓ} = ρ_{N+1-k}(t)_{ℓℓ}*  =  ρ_{N+1-k}(t)_{ℓℓ}   (diagonal of Hermitian ρ is real).

Populations are identical pointwise.

**Off-diagonal (ℓ ≠ m):**

    |ρ_k(t)_{ℓm}|² = |ρ_{N+1-k}(t)_{ℓm}|².

Coherence moduli are identical; the relative phase differs by (−1)^{ℓ+m} composed with complex conjugation.

Any observable expressible as a function of populations and coherence moduli is therefore K-partner invariant. Mirror-pair MI(a, N-1-a) constructs the 2-site reduced density matrix ρ_{ab} from p_a, p_b, and ρ_{ab}; its von Neumann entropy depends on eigenvalues that depend on |ρ_{ab}|² (the coherence enters quadratically through det), so MI is invariant. The PTF-analog Σ_ℓ log π_ℓ depends only on populations and is invariant.   ∎

**Phase-sensitive observables.** Re(ρ_{ab}(t)) for fixed a, b is **not** invariant: the sign (−1)^{a+b} composed with complex conjugation generally flips Re. The receiver-menu folding under K-partnership therefore applies to |·|²-observables only.

### Lemma 5 (K-Invariance of Z-dephasing dissipator)

L_D acts diagonally per matrix element: L_D[τ]_{ℓm} = −γ_eff(ℓ, m) τ_{ℓm}. Then

    (K L_D[K σ K] K)_{ℓm} = (−1)^{ℓ+m} L_D[K σ K]_{ℓm} = (−1)^{ℓ+m}(−γ_eff)(K σ K)_{ℓm}
                          = (−1)^{ℓ+m}(−γ_eff)(−1)^{ℓ+m} σ_{ℓm} = −γ_eff σ_{ℓm} = L_D[σ]_{ℓm}.

So L_D = K L_D(K · K) K, i.e., the dissipator commutes with K-conjugation. This holds for **any** profile {γ_ℓ}, including non-uniform Z-dephasing. Lemma 5 is what allows L4 to absorb arbitrary γ_ℓ-profiles without breaking partner identity.   ∎

## AZ classification

K is a **chiral unitary symmetry**: K = K† = K⁻¹ with K² = I and KHK = −H. In Altland-Zirnbauer (ten-fold-way) terminology this is called *chiral* symmetry, often denoted S in the condensed-matter literature. K is **not** time-reversal (which is anti-unitary) and **not** particle-hole (also anti-unitary). The recurrent terminology error of calling KHK = −H "time reversal" is the reason the proof above explicitly tracks T = complex conjugation as a separate ingredient.

When H is real, the additional anti-unitary T (complex conjugation) gives T² = +I and [T, H] = 0. Combined with chiral S = K, the AZ classification is **class BDI**:

| Symmetry  | Operator        | Property              |
|-----------|-----------------|------------------------|
| TRS       | T (complex conj.) | T² = +I, [T, H] = 0  |
| PHS       | T·S             | (T·S)² = +I            |
| Chiral    | S = K           | S² = +I, {S, H} = 0    |

For **complex** hopping (Peierls phase, t_ℓ ∈ ℂ\ℝ), TRS breaks and the system drops to **class AIII** (chiral only). Spectrum inversion (L2) still holds, but the observable identity (L4) requires the complex conjugation step and fails: ρ_k(t) and ρ_{N+1-k}(t) have inverted spectrum but the populations may differ in time. This boundary between BDI and AIII is the structural reason the partner-identity table in HANDSHAKE_ALGEBRA needs *real* H, not just *bipartite* H.

The break cases show all non-self pairs failing, as predicted. The NNN test (Test 4) numerically confirms Lemma 1's prediction: NNN bonds connect same-sublattice sites (i, i+2) where (−1)^{i+(i+2)} = +1, so KHK ≠ −H on those terms, and the partner identity collapses at the same magnitude as the on-site-potential case.

**BDI/AIII split (Test 5).** The Peierls-phase test runs two diagnostics in parallel:

| Diagnostic                                              | Real H (BDI)        | Complex H (AIII)    | Lemma confirmed    |
|---------------------------------------------------------|---------------------|---------------------|--------------------|
| Spectrum inversion: max \|E_k + E_{N+1-k}\|             | 1.3 · 10⁻¹⁵         | 1.1 · 10⁻¹⁵         | L2 holds in both   |

Spectrum inversion E_k + E_{N+1-k} = 0 holds at machine precision **even with complex hopping** because Lemma 1 (KHK = −H) is independent of T-symmetry. But the trajectory identity (Lemma 4) fails because its proof relies on the complex-conjugation step ρ_{N+1-k}(t) = (K ρ_k(t) K)*, which requires H = H*. The Peierls test therefore numerically separates the two layers of the proof: the chiral structure (S = K, AIII) gives spectrum inversion alone; the full BDI structure (S + T) is needed for observable identity.

**Not yet tested numerically:** multi-excitation sector (open question, ZZ-interaction-induced on-site potential), Heisenberg/XXZ Δ ≠ 0 on open chain (predicted boundary breakdown via deg-discontinuity).

## Scope and limitations

**Valid for:**

- Single-excitation sector of any bipartite NN-hopping Hamiltonian with real t_ℓ (uniform or non-uniform).
- Arbitrary site-dependent Z-dephasing γ_ℓ.
- Open boundary condition; periodic boundary with even N (preserves bipartite structure).
- Any |·|²-based observable on mirror-pairs (a, N-1-a): MI, classical correlation, log-purity, fidelity to symmetric reference states.

**Does NOT cover:**

- Multi-excitation sectors. Each fixed-particle-number sector still reduces to a tight-binding problem in the appropriate Fock space, but the on-site interaction terms generated by ZZ-coupling generally break L1 even on periodic chains.
- Phase-sensitive observables (Re(ρ_{ab}), Im(ρ_{ab}), holomorphic functions of ρ).
- Hamiltonians with on-site potential, NNN hopping, or non-bipartite topology.
- Complex hopping (no T-symmetry; only spectrum inversion survives).
- Heisenberg/XXZ Δ ≠ 0 on **open** chains (deg-discontinuity at boundaries).

**Verified:**

- N = 9 single-excitation, five H-classes (uniform J, non-uniform J, +V_ℓ on-site, +NNN J′, Peierls complex hopping), two γ regimes (0 and 0.1).
- BDI/AIII split numerically separated: spectrum inversion (L2) holds in both classes; trajectory identity (L4) requires real H (BDI).

**Open:**

- Extension to multi-excitation sectors.
- Heisenberg/XXZ Δ ≠ 0 on open chain (predicted boundary breakdown).
- Larger N (limited only by the cost of the single-excitation reduction; trivial in the matrix-free regime).

## Relation to other results

- **F1 / [MIRROR_SYMMETRY_PROOF](MIRROR_SYMMETRY_PROOF.md) (Π).** F1's Π is a Pauli-string conjugation operator on the 4ᴺ operator space; it maps total XY-weight w to N − w via the per-site swap I ↔ X, Y ↔ iZ, Z ↔ iY. F1's Π is **not** a Hilbert-space operator and does not act on sites; it acts on observables (operators), not on states. K, by contrast, is a Hilbert-space involution on the 2ᴺ space (or its single-excitation N-dim sector). The two symmetries are independent involutions of distinct objects.

- **F71 / [PROOF_C1_MIRROR_SYMMETRY](PROOF_C1_MIRROR_SYMMETRY.md) (R).** F71's R is the *spatial reflection* R |1_ℓ⟩ = |1_{N-1-ℓ}⟩. R fixes each F65 mode up to a phase: R ψ_k = (−1)^{k+1} ψ_k. K, by contrast, **swaps** modes: K ψ_k = ψ_{N+1-k}. R and K are distinct Hilbert-space involutions that happen to coexist on the uniform palindromic chain. R requires uniform J (translation-symmetric reflection); K requires only bipartiteness (much weaker).

- **F65 (bonding-mode spectrum).** The arithmetic identity α_k = α_{N+1-k} from sin²(kπ/(N+1)) = sin²((N+1-k)π/(N+1)) is the spectral consequence of K. F65 stated this as a property of the closed-form formula; the K theorem identifies it as a structural symmetry of H robust under non-uniform J (where the closed-form sin² no longer applies).

- **HANDSHAKE_ALGEBRA.** The K-partnership of receiver tuples h ↔ h̃ = (N, N+1-k, t, basis) is the operational consequence: the receiver menu folds from N to ⌈N/2⌉ distinct entries under K-partner equivalence. This proof grounds the table in section "The second mirror: K as the partnership axis".
