# The Outbound Arc: PARKED (2026-07-06)

This folder holds **outbound adapters**: drafts that translate a repository
result into an external research community's language and open problem. They are
outreach *packaging*, not new science.

## Status: the whole arc is parked (Tom, 2026-07-06)

None of these has been sent to any community, and none is outreach-ready. The
arc is parked, for three reasons:

1. **No outreach is planned or in progress.** Contacting a community is gated on
   Tom's explicit call, which has not been given.
2. **One adapter proved hollow on review.** The concentrator adapter
   (`SELECTIVE_DECOUPLING_SELECTION_RULE.md`, S2) went through 6 empty-session
   review rounds (2026-07-05); its hardware evidence turned out not to test the
   mechanism it was selling (the "natural concentrator" was a T1 sink outside
   the theorem's scope; the engineered-sink test was artifact-dominated and did
   not settle the question). See its own banner. The other three have **not**
   had that review, so they are treated as **unvalidated**.
3. **Nothing of the science is lost by parking.** Each adapter is a translation;
   every underlying result lives in its home and is unaffected:

| Adapter | Community | The result actually lives in |

| STATE_TRANSFER_DECAY_STRUCTURE (S1) | quantum state transfer | the decay-rate / spectral-pairing Tier-1 theorems (machine-verified); benchmarks are simulation |

## Un-park condition (per adapter)

An adapter comes back only when there is (a) an actual reason to contact its
community, (b) a clean empty-review round on it (referee / record-audit /
mathematician / physicist, per the `becoming-your-own-outside` skill), and
(c) primary-source verification of every external citation.

## Why the files stay here

Parked in place, not moved: many docs, the `THE_LABEL_MAP` translation index,
and the OpenArcs ledger link to these files, and the repo treats links as its
map. This is a **status change, not a removal**; the inbound links stay intact.
