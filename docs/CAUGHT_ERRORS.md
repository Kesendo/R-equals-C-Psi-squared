# Caught Errors

A record of errors caught before (or shortly after) they landed, and of review near-misses that taught us something, so the repo remembers and does not repeat them. This is intellectual honesty as a first-class artifact, in the spirit of `recovered/`, `RetractedClaim`, and "negative results matter": a visible self-correction record builds credibility, it does not cost it.

Maintained by the `reviewing-before-it-lands` review workflow. Markdown now; will graduate to a typed `CaughtErrorsRegistry` (a world-root section, count-locked) once the shape settles. Append-only.

Each entry: **date | artifact | what was wrong | why | how caught | fix | anchor**. "Caught error" includes both real errors in an artifact and *review process* near-misses (a perspectival truth almost "fixed", a false unity almost shipped), since those repeat just as easily.

## 2026-06-20 — `PROOF_F112_NONHERMITIAN_UNIVERSAL_N.md`: physical-scope overclaim (commutator ≠ physical NH generator)

The first real catch of the `reviewing-before-it-lands` workflow, on a *randomly chosen* proof.

- **What was wrong:** the proof claimed F112's non-Hermitian extension puts "PT-symmetric and gain-loss Lindblad systems on the same footing" and holds for "any Lindblad-form Liouvillian" with non-Hermitian H.
- **Why:** the theorem is about the **commutator** superoperator −i[H,·] for any matrix H (a structural ‖·‖² identity). The physical generator of PT-symmetric / gain-loss / post-selection dynamics is −i(Hρ−ρH†) = −i[A,ρ] + {B,ρ} (H=A+iB) — a different operator (anticommutator in the anti-Hermitian part) — and the balance can fail for it, content-dependently (sharpened same day: it is the commutator–drain cross-term carried by single-site-Z detuning in H; a uniform-damping bond-Hamiltonian chain is itself BALANCED, and the "132/270" was a random-H artifact). Every empirical anchor built the commutator (`kron(Id, H.T)`), never the physical generator.
- **How caught:** the workflow's 3-lens panel on the random proof. The physics-first+grounding lens computed that the physical-generator balance fails; I grounded the −i[A,ρ]+{B,ρ} algebra myself (did not trust the agent); the math lens confirmed the verifier only ever builds the commutator; the cold auditor confirmed the counts (the "559,912 vs 524,800" I flagged was a non-issue — two valid counts, perspectival, no fix). Symmetric gate: NOT perspectival (two genuinely different operators), a **genuine break** in the scope claim (a false unity, "commutator result = physical PT/gain-loss result").
- **Fix:** restricted the proof's prose to the commutator −i[H,·] (scope anchor in the abstract + motivation / intro / §f / corollary / status); stated the physical-generator boundary as the useful fact. Corrected the seed in `reflections/POLARITY_COORDINATES.md` (Probe 1 conclusion). The math/lemmas were and remain correct (machine zero). Siblings (parent proof, cross-dephase proof, `LindbladBitBPiBalance.cs`, F-registry, enumeration experiment) verified FINE — all "non-Hermitian H" already in the commutator sense. Fresh-lens re-review confirmed the fix sound (and caught this very ledger entry being a dangling citation before it was written).
- **Anchor:** `docs/proofs/PROOF_F112_NONHERMITIAN_UNIVERSAL_N.md`.
- **Lesson:** a proof can be mathematically airtight yet overclaim its physical scope — the math being right does not make the named application right. Ground the OBJECT (which operator) before believing the scope.
- **Opened a direction, then closed it (generative pass + its own gate, same day):** the generative wing proposed a reframe (the asymmetry = chirality of the un-recycled drain, circular Stokes V of M); its own gate-first probe `simulations/_f112_nojump_cancellation_gate.py` REFUTED it — the un-recycled drain has no chirality (the no-jump generator of any physical spin chain is balanced). ## 2026-06-20 — F91/F92/F93 anti-palindromic family: "Z₄ on the parameter vector" is Klein V₄; a backwards sum/difference statement; F91's "multiset" overclaim

Second random-proof catch of the `reviewing-before-it-lands` workflow (F92 drawn by dice), with a systemic blast radius across the γ/J/h family. The core spectral results are correct and bit-exact; every defect is in the framing/decoration.

- **What was wrong (three distinct defects):**
  1. *Group structure.* F91/F92/F93 each frame the anti-palindromic parameter reshuffle R₉₀ : P ↦ 2·P_avg − F71(P) as the "90° element of a Pi2-Z₄ acting on the parameter vector, with i² = the 180° palindromic mirror F71, i⁴ = 1." On the parameter vector R₉₀ is an **involution** (R₉₀² = identity ≠ F71), so ⟨F71, R₉₀⟩ is the **Klein four-group V₄ = Z₂×Z₂**, not a cyclic Z₄; there is no order-4 element on the parameters (at N=4, dim(P_anti)=1, so no real order-4 map can exist). The genuine i⁴ = 1 / 90° quarter-turn lives only on the **operator side** (F80's Spec(M) = ±2i·Spec(H), i² = −1; `NinetyDegreeMirrorMemoryClaim`) — correct and untouched; R₉₀ is its order-2 parameter-side *shadow*.
  2. *Backwards statement.* F91 (abstract + §Pi2 prose) and F92 said R₉₀ "flips the pair-difference while preserving/fixing the pair-sum." Backwards: R₉₀ **preserves each pair-difference and reflects each pair-sum** about 2·P_avg (S ↦ 4·P_avg − S). F91's own Step 7 (Eq. 13) already had it right, so F91 internally contradicted itself (line 25/68 vs 200).
  3. *F91 content overclaim.* F91 Step 6 concluded the diagonal-block spectrum depends "only through the **multiset** of pair-sums {S_l}". It depends on the **indexed** pair-sums S_l (≙ P_sym as a vector): by F91's own Eq. 7a each diagonal element sums S_l over a specific differing-site set, so reassigning which site-pair carries which sum changes the blocks. The anti-palindromic corollary survives because there all S_l = 2·P_avg (a constant vector, where multiset = assignment).
  Plus: F92's abstract said "the eigenvalues stand still" / "the spectrum keeps the symmetric half of J" unscoped — read as the physical full-L spectrum, which moves substantially on the orbit.
- **Why:** the operator-side i⁴=1 was transported wholesale onto the parameter vector; "multiset" was a tidier-but-false weakening of what Eq. 7a proves; the abstract attached "eigenvalues" to the compressed diagonal blocks, not to L.
- **How caught:** the parallel lens panel on the dice-drawn proof — mathematical-review (Z₂-not-Z₄ + its own N=6 multiset counterexample), physics-first (full-L over-scope + the joint-popcount / F100-first-order right-sizing), survey (systemic blast radius + the F91 self-contradiction). The Z₄ label, the backwards statement, and the multiset claim are **genuine breaks** (false group, false direction, false generality), each grounded.
